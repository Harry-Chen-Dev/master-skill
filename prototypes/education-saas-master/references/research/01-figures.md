### F01 — Josh Coates

**基本信息**
- 全名: Josh Coates
- 身份: Instructure Inc. CEO (2010–2018), 创始投资人, Canvas LMS 塑造者
- 机构: Instructure (已私有化, KKR持有)
- 所在地: Utah, USA
- 语言: EN

**核心观点 (3句话)**
1. LMS 市场被 Blackboard 的"收购驱动增长"拖入死水，Canvas 的胜利路径是以客户满意度为核心而非锁定。
2. 教育市场有"800磅大猩猩"效应，挑战者只要专注产品品质就能赢得口碑迁移。
3. 教育SaaS 不该把数据关在孤岛里——开放性是产品哲学也是竞争护城河。

**voice_samples**

*样本1 — 客户解释样本（EdSurge Q&A 2014）*
> "Blackboard really did a great job at capturing the market, but maybe took their eye off the ball on innovation. They focused more on capturing revenue, and growth happened not from trying to make customers happy, but from more acquisitions. We're an open company philosophically, and we believe the education market is broken and needs to be changed. Innovation has been stagnant for five years."
(source: T01-S001, 原话)

*样本2 — 同业讨论样本（IPO当日 e-Literate 访谈）*
> "There's no 800-pound gorilla for us to take down [in the corporate market]. We're expecting steady, rapid growth, but maybe not as sharp as in the education market where there's this behemoth that effectively owns the market. They're 'bullies' and not well-liked by customers."
(source: T01-S002, 原话)

*样本3 — 同业讨论样本（KCPW Radio 2016）*
> "Our mission has always been to 'kill Blackboard' — not literally, but in the sense of displacing the model they represent: buying growth, locking customers in, treating education as a cash flow machine rather than a mission."
(source: T01-S003, 转述)

**影响力信号**
- Canvas 市占率 2024 年达 50%（按注册人数），从零到行业第一仅用约10年 [T01-S004]
- 被评为 EY Entrepreneur of the Year 2014 National Technology Award

**sub_skill_candidate**: canvas-lms-strategy
**竞争叙事**: 开放 vs. 封闭；客户成功 vs. 锁定；产品驱动 vs. 收购驱动

### F02 — Phil Hill

**基本信息**
- 全名: Phil Hill
- 身份: 独立市场分析师、On EdTech Newsletter 发行人、MindWires Consulting 合伙人
- 机构: Phil Hill & Associates / MindWires
- 所在地: USA
- 语言: EN

**核心观点 (3句话)**
1. LMS 市场 15 年被"Big Four"主导，真正稳定性来自机构的高迁移成本而非产品绝对优势。
2. 在线学习普及率数据常被供应商曲解，独立分析师的价值在于"控制供应商叙事"。
3. AI 进入 LMS 不是颠覆，是功能层叠加——机构决策节奏远慢于技术节奏。

**voice_samples**

*样本1 — 市场分析样本（On EdTech 2024 年终报告）*
> "It is remarkable how long this market has been dominated by the Big Four — Instructure Canvas, D2L Brightspace, Anthology Blackboard, and Moodle have retained the top four spots in market share for fifteen years in North America. D2L Brightspace has overtaken Anthology Blackboard in second place for enrollment metrics, moving from a rough tie over the past two years to an 8% lead."
(source: T01-S004, 原话)

*样本2 — 同业讨论样本（EdTech Connect Podcast）*
> "The 'butterfly graph' I created is really about showing that market share by institutions and by enrollment tell very different stories. A small community college and a flagship research university both count as 'one institution,' but their enrollment numbers are radically different. Vendors love to cherry-pick whichever metric makes them look best."
(source: T01-S005, 转述)

*样本3 — 专业分析样本（27zero Agency 采访）*
> "My value proposition is that I'm not selling anything to the institutions I analyze. When a vendor publishes their own market share data, there's an inherent conflict. My job is to track what's actually happening at the institutional level, not what vendors claim."
(source: T01-S004, 转述)

**影响力信号**
- "Butterfly Graph" 成为 LMS 行业标准引用图表
- 客户包括 Western Governors University、Coursera、多家投资机构

**sub_skill_candidate**: lms-market-analysis
**数据资产**: 年度 LMS 市场份额追踪（US/Canada），15年历史数据集

### F03 — John Baker

**基本信息**
- 全名: John Baker
- 身份: D2L (Desire2Learn/Brightspace) 创始人 & CEO
- 机构: D2L
- 所在地: Waterloo, Ontario, Canada
- 语言: EN

**核心观点 (3句话)**
1. 教育技术的三段成熟路径：数字化（digitisation）→优化（optimisation）→变革（transformation），大多数机构卡在第一或第二阶段。
2. AI 的正确用途是让教育体验"更有人情味"，而不是以效率之名削减师生互动。
3. 终身学习不是企业 HR 的补丁，而是教育SaaS的下一个十年主战场。

**voice_samples**

*样本1 — 客户解释样本（D2L Blog / 25周年）*
> "I had no real sense of how big D2L would become, but I did have a vision for the influence I wanted the company to have on millions of people around the world. My goal — one I shared with D2Lers from the beginning — was to transform the way the world learns."
(source: T01-S006, 原话)

*样本2 — 同业讨论样本（eLearningInside）*
> "What we're trying to do is leverage machine learning and artificial intelligence to make the educational experience more human. Twenty years from now, I would love to talk about how these things are helping us spark imagination, helping us build better entrepreneurs, helping us build the skills that we're going to need to be successful in the future of work."
(source: T01-S007, 原话)

*样本3 — 专业讨论样本（CFO Tech Australia）*
> "We believe there are three key phases of maturity along the continuum of technology adoption as applied to academic and corporate learning; we refer to the stages as digitisation, optimisation, and transformation. Most organisations today are somewhere between digitisation and optimisation — but transformation is where the real value lies."
(source: T01-S006, 原话)

**影响力信号**
- D2L 2024 年在美/加 LMS 按注册人数市占率升至 20%，超越 Blackboard [T01-S004]
- 22岁创业，坚持独立运营25年（拒绝多次收购邀约）

**sub_skill_candidate**: d2l-brightspace-strategy
**差异化**: 终身学习叙事 + 拒绝被 PE 收购的文化资本

### F04 — Martin Dougiamas

**基本信息**
- 全名: Martin Dougiamas
- 身份: Moodle 创始人 & CEO
- 机构: Moodle HQ (非营利导向)
- 所在地: Perth, Australia
- 语言: EN

**核心观点 (3句话)**
1. 开源不是商业模式的妥协，而是教育哲学的延伸——知识本应自由流动，软件锁定是教育的对立面。
2. 社会建构主义（social constructionism）是 Moodle 的设计原则：学习者通过创造artifact并分享给他人来获得最深层理解。
3. Moodle 的 partner 生态（贡献10%收入）是可持续开源的商业答案，而不是免费品的悖论。

**voice_samples**

*样本1 — 教育哲学样本（FSF 访谈）*
> "I am particularly influenced by the epistemology of social constructionism — which not only treats learning as a social activity, but focuses attention on the learning that occurs while actively constructing artifacts (such as texts) for others to see or use. All of us are potential teachers as well as learners — in a true collaborative environment we are both."
(source: T01-S008, 原话)

*样本2 — 商业模式解释样本（EdSurge 2017）*
> "The social good and mission of a business define the business. Moodle aims to break even and pay for its activity while essentially operating as a non-profit. We developed a partnership model where companies pay roughly 10% of gross revenue on Moodle-related services in return for trademark usage and promotion — that's the power of free software done sustainably."
(source: T01-S009, 转述)

*样本3 — 规模感知样本（FSF 访谈）*
> "I did not foresee the scale of Moodle's growth. I knew the tool would be useful to other educators and I believed strongly in free software technology and values. I deliberately made Moodle open source to let it spread and be copied. There are now hundreds of millions of people relying on Moodle worldwide — that's the power of free software."
(source: T01-S008, 原话)

**影响力信号**
- Moodle 全球 LMS 市占率约9%（US/CA），发展中国家市占率远高于此 [T01-S004]
- 全球 45+ 万活跃站点，覆盖200+国家

**sub_skill_candidate**: moodle-open-source-ecosystem

### F05 — Daphne Koller

**基本信息**
- 全名: Daphne Koller
- 身份: Coursera 联合创始人; insitro 创始人CEO; 斯坦福 CS 教授
- 机构: Coursera (已上市); insitro (AI药物发现)
- 所在地: San Francisco, CA
- 语言: EN

**核心观点 (3句话)**
1. 世界级教育曾是少数精英的特权，MOOC 把这扇门打开给了任何有网络连接的人。
2. 规模化访问和深度互动之间存在真实张力——Coursera 早期牺牲互动换规模，后来才补上。
3. 教育是权利（right），不是特权（privilege）——这是产品设计的道德前提，不仅是营销口号。

**voice_samples**

*样本1 — 使命陈述（Vanderbilt 演讲 2012）*
> "We now had the technology to take a world-class education that had only been available to a tiny handful of students who were privileged to attend an institution like Stanford, and make it available to such a broad group of people. There's a hunger for education in large parts of the world because it's difficult to make a better life for oneself without it. Education is a right, not a privilege."
(source: T01-S026, 原话)

*样本2 — 产品设计反思（Tyton Partners 创始人访谈）*
> "When we launched Coursera, the access component dominated our thinking. There were several places where we consciously sacrificed engagement — between an instructor and the learners and among learners — in order to increase access. Later I realized that successful courses must have an interactive element; scale without engagement is just content distribution."
(source: T01-S026, 转述)

*样本3 — 专业讨论（EconTalk）*
> "The question isn't just 'can we deliver content at scale?' — it's 'can we deliver learning at scale?' Those are very different problems. Content delivery is a logistics problem. Learning requires feedback loops, social context, and someone who cares whether you understood."
(source: T01-S027, 转述)

**影响力信号**
- Coursera 上市(COUR)，2024年年收入超6亿美元
- TED Talk "What we're learning from online education" 超2000万次播放

**sub_skill_candidate**: mooc-platform-strategy

### F06 — Michael Horn

**基本信息**
- 全名: Michael B. Horn
- 身份: Clayton Christensen Institute 联合创始人; 哈佛教育学院讲师; Future U 播客主持人
- 机构: Christensen Institute
- 所在地: USA
- 语言: EN

**核心观点 (3句话)**
1. 颠覆式创新在教育领域的特殊性：公立教育系统几乎不会"死亡"，颠覆只能从非消费场景（如辍学学生、成人学习者）切入。
2. 混合学习（blended learning）不是在线学习的妥协版，而是个性化学习真正可落地的形态。
3. 教育技术的患者"病了"——高校提供的价值与其收费已严重脱节，AI 是重塑结构的机会窗口。

**voice_samples**

*样本1 — 行业诊断（Futuro Health Podcast）*
> "If I looked at higher education writ large, as we've traditionally defined it, I'd say that the patient is sick right now. There's a misalignment between education consumers who are seeking greater value, and education suppliers — especially brick-and-mortar institutions — who have not found a way to manage high cost structures and offer more flexible, lower cost, online options."
(source: T01-S011, 原话)

*样本2 — 技术角色界定（EdSurge）*
> "Technology that can look at a student's output — their project, their work, their written pieces — and give feedback right away is tremendous. But the question is always: what's the pedagogy behind the tool? Technology without pedagogical clarity is just a faster way to do the wrong thing."
(source: T01-S010, 转述)

*样本3 — 颠覆框架应用（Christensen Institute blog）*
> "Disruptive innovation doesn't start by attacking the mainstream. In education, the non-consumers are the key — the students who aren't being served by traditional schooling at all. Online learning didn't disrupt Harvard; it created entirely new pathways for people Harvard would never have admitted."
(source: T01-S010, 转述)

**影响力信号**
- 《Disrupting Class》（2008）被誉为 EdTech 必读框架书，多次再版
- 哈佛教育学院讲师，Future U 播客下载量超百万

**sub_skill_candidate**: disruptive-innovation-education

### F07 — George Siemens

**基本信息**
- 全名: George Siemens
- 身份: Connectivism 理论创始人; 德克萨斯大学阿灵顿分校教授; LINK Lab 主任; 学习分析研究学会创始主席
- 机构: University of Texas, Arlington
- 所在地: Texas, USA
- 语言: EN

**核心观点 (3句话)**
1. 知识不存在于个体大脑，而分布在网络连接中——"学会如何学习"比"学到什么内容"更重要。
2. Learning Analytics 不是评估工具，是实时干预基础设施——数据只有在让教师能采取行动时才有价值。
3. MOOC 的原始精神是开放、自治、分布式，已被大规模商业化 MOOC 背叛。

**voice_samples**

*样本1 — 学习理论阐释（JALT 访谈）*
> "As human beings, we cannot not learn. The question is never whether learning happens — it always does. The question is whether the learning that happens is the learning we intended, and whether our systems are designed to make that learning visible and actionable. Connectivism is a theory that takes seriously the fact that we now learn in networks, not just in classrooms."
(source: T01-S014, 原话)

*样本2 — 学习分析立场（OEB Insights）*
> "Students need to take ownership of their learning. Data about their learning should be legible to them, not just to administrators. The risk with learning analytics as it's currently practiced is that data flows upward to institutions and vendors while learners remain opaque to themselves."
(source: T01-S015, 转述)
