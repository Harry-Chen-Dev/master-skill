## Glossary 详细卡片

### Tier 1 详细条目

---

#### T1-01: LMS (Learning Management System)

- **type**: Acronym/Platform
- **zh**: 学习管理系统
- **definition**: 用于管理、交付、追踪和报告在线学习内容的软件平台；是教育SaaS的核心基础设施。Core functions include course delivery, enrollment management, gradebook, progress tracking, and compliance reporting. [T06-S012]
- **context**: In higher ed (US 2023), Canvas holds ~41% market share, Blackboard ~17%, Moodle and D2L ~16% each. Enterprise L&D uses Cornerstone, SAP SuccessFactors. K-12 uses Schoology, Canvas, Google Classroom. [T06-S012]
- **outsider-tell**: Confusing LMS with SIS ("把学籍管理系统叫成LMS") or with LXP. LMS = content delivery + compliance tracking; SIS = enrollment + transcripts + scheduling; LXP = personalized AI-driven pathways. Another tell: calling every EdTech platform an "LMS."
- **related**: SIS, LXP, SCORM, LTI, xAPI, grade passback

---

#### T1-02: SIS (Student Information System)

- **type**: Acronym/Platform
- **zh**: 学生信息系统
- **definition**: 管理学生数据的行政系统，涵盖入学注册、成绩单、排课、考勤、学费。Distinct from LMS: SIS is the system of record for student identity and academic history; LMS is the system of delivery for learning content. [T06-S013]
- **context**: Also called Student Management System (SMS) or School Administration Software. Major vendors: PowerSchool (K-12), Infinite Campus, Ellucian Banner (higher ed), Oracle Student Cloud. In China: 教务系统/教务ERP.
- **outsider-tell**: Treating SIS and LMS as the same system. SIS holds authoritative records (who is enrolled, what grades were earned); LMS holds learning activities (what content was consumed). OneRoster API bridges the two.
- **related**: LMS, OneRoster, rostering, FERPA, ERP

---

#### T1-03: LXP (Learning Experience Platform)

- **type**: Acronym/Platform
- **zh**: 学习体验平台
- **definition**: A learner-centric platform that aggregates content from multiple sources and uses AI/ML to recommend personalized learning paths. Contrasts with LMS: LXP is pull (learner-driven) vs LMS push (admin-assigned). [T06-S012]
- **context**: Term coined ~2017 (Josh Bersin). Major vendors: Degreed, EdCast, Cornerstone EdX, LinkedIn Learning. Growing in corporate L&D. In higher ed, LXP features are being absorbed into next-gen LMS.
- **outsider-tell**: Using LXP and LMS interchangeably. Key differentiator: LXP = learner-initiated exploration + social learning + external content aggregation; LMS = institutionally-mandated course completion + compliance tracking.
- **related**: LMS, adaptive learning, PLG

---

#### T1-04: ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)

- **type**: Acronym/SaaS Metric
- **zh**: 年经常性收入
- **definition**: 来自订阅合同的年化经常性收入，排除一次性费用（实施费、培训费）。Primary top-line metric for SaaS valuation. ARR = MRR × 12 for monthly contracts; for annual contracts, divide total contract value by years. [T04-S003]
- **context**: EdTech SaaS ARR benchmarks: seed stage <$1M, Series A $2-10M, Series B $10-50M. Education-specific consideration: district budget cycles (July–June) and semester-based churn create lumpy ARR recognition.
- **outsider-tell**: Including one-time implementation fees or professional services in ARR. Also: confusing ARR with revenue — ARR is a forward-looking metric (contracted future value), not recognized revenue.
- **related**: MRR, NRR, logo retention, churn rate

---

#### T1-05: NRR (Net Revenue Retention)

- **type**: Acronym/SaaS Metric
- **zh**: 净收入留存率 / 净留存率
- **definition**: Measures revenue retained from existing customers including expansions, contractions, and churn. NRR > 100% means existing cohort grows without new logos. Formula: (Beginning ARR + Expansions - Contractions - Churned) / Beginning ARR × 100. [T04-S003]
- **context**: World-class EdTech SaaS: NRR >120%. Median B2B SaaS: ~100-110%. Education has structural NRR risk: district budget cuts, enrollment decline (US K-12 post-COVID), policy changes (双减 crushed K12 tutoring NRR). Seat expansion (more licenses) and module upsell drive NRR >100%.
- **outsider-tell**: Confusing NRR (dollar retention) with logo retention (customer count retention). A company can have 90% logo retention but 110% NRR if remaining customers expand seats. Also called NDR (Net Dollar Retention) — same metric.
- **related**: ARR, logo retention, churn rate, seat expansion

---

#### T1-06: Multi-tenancy

- **type**: Term/Architecture
- **zh**: 多租户架构
- **definition**: A software architecture where a single application instance serves multiple customers (tenants), with logical data separation between tenants. Contrasts with single-tenancy (dedicated instance per customer). [T06-S018]
- **context**: Three common patterns in EdTech SaaS: (1) Silo — separate database per tenant, highest isolation, highest cost; (2) Pool — shared database, row-level tenant ID, lowest cost; (3) Bridge — shared app, separate schemas. EdTech buyers (districts, universities) increasingly require data isolation guarantees and FERPA compliance certifications.
- **outsider-tell**: Assuming multi-tenant = less secure. Properly implemented pool-model multi-tenancy with row-level security can be as secure as silo. The outsider mistake is equating "shared infrastructure" with "shared data."
- **related**: data isolation, FERPA, SOC 2 Type II, SSO

---

#### T1-07: Enrollment funnel

- **type**: Term/Higher Ed
- **zh**: 招生漏斗
- **definition**: The sequential stages a prospective student moves through from initial awareness to enrolled matriculant. Typical stages: Inquiry → Application → Admitted → Deposited → Enrolled. [T06-S017]
- **context**: Key metrics at each stage: inquiry-to-application rate, admit rate, yield rate (admitted→enrolled), melt rate (deposited but doesn't show up). EdTech CRM platforms (Salesforce Education Cloud, Slate, Hobsons) automate funnel nurture. In K-12, enrollment funnel = lead capture for private schools and tutoring centers.
- **outsider-tell**: Using "yield rate" to mean offer-acceptance rate only — in higher ed, yield rate specifically = (enrolled students) / (admitted students). "Melt" is the loss between deposit payment and first-day attendance, which many outsiders don't know exists as a distinct problem.
- **related**: yield rate, melt rate, SIS, CRM

---

#### T1-08: SCORM (Sharable Content Object Reference Model)

- **type**: Acronym/Standard
- **zh**: 共享内容对象参考模型
- **definition**: A collection of standards for web-based eLearning content, maintained by ADL (Advanced Distributed Learning Initiative, U.S. DoD). Defines content packaging (ZIP), runtime API communication between content and LMS, and data model (completion status, score, time). [T06-S001]
- **context**: Two main versions: SCORM 1.2 (2001, most widely deployed legacy standard) and SCORM 2004 4th Ed (2009, improved sequencing but low adoption). As of 2025, SCORM 1.2 remains the most-deployed eLearning standard despite being 20+ years old. Successor: xAPI for modern tracking; cmi5 for LMS-hosted xAPI content. [T06-S023]
- **outsider-tell**: Saying "SCORM 2.0" — it doesn't exist. The successor to SCORM 2004 is xAPI (not SCORM 3.0 or SCORM 2.0). Also: SCORM tracks "pass/fail + score" only; it cannot track mobile, offline, or non-browser activities.
- **related**: xAPI, cmi5, LMS, LTI, ADL

---

#### T1-09: xAPI (Experience API)

- **type**: Acronym/Standard
- **zh**: 体验API / Tin Can API
- **definition**: A learning technology specification enabling any learning experience to be recorded in an LRS using actor-verb-object statements ("John completed Module 3"). Developed by ADL (Project Tin Can via Rustici Software, 2013). Standardized as IEEE 9274.1.1-2023 (xAPI 2.0). [T06-S002]
- **context**: Unlike SCORM, xAPI: (1) works outside the browser (mobile, games, simulations, on-the-job); (2) stores data in an LRS, not just the LMS; (3) tracks granular behavior (not just pass/fail). The LRS can be standalone (Watershed, SCORM Cloud) or embedded in LMS. cmi5 is the xAPI profile that governs how content is launched from an LMS context.
- **outsider-tell**: Calling it "Tin Can" in a formal/sales context — the nickname is informal; the spec name is xAPI. Also: assuming xAPI "replaces" SCORM everywhere — in practice both coexist; SCORM 1.2 still dominates legacy content libraries.
- **related**: SCORM, cmi5, LRS, LMS, ADL

---

#### T1-10: LTI (Learning Tools Interoperability)

- **type**: Acronym/Standard
- **zh**: 学习工具互操作标准
- **definition**: A 1EdTech standard enabling third-party tools (publishers, assessment platforms, video tools) to embed securely within an LMS with SSO and grade passback. LTI 1.3 uses OAuth 2.0 + JSON Web Tokens for auth. LTI Advantage adds three services: AGS (grade passback), NRPS (names/roles), Deep Linking. [T06-S003]
- **context**: LTI 1.1 (older, widely deployed) uses OAuth 1.0 shared secret — less secure. LTI 1.3 / LTI Advantage is the current standard (2019+). Without LTI, every publisher integration requires custom API work. With LTI, a certified tool plugs into any LTI-compliant LMS (Canvas, Blackboard, Moodle) in <1 day.
- **outsider-tell**: Confusing LTI with an API — LTI is a launch protocol (identity + context passing), not a full data API. Also: thinking LTI 1.1 and LTI 1.3 are compatible — they are not; LTI 1.3 requires re-registration.
- **related**: SCORM, xAPI, grade passback, SSO, 1EdTech

---

#### T1-11: OneRoster

- **type**: Standard
- **zh**: 名册数据交换标准
- **definition**: A 1EdTech standard for exchanging roster data (students, teachers, classes, enrollments, grades) between SIS and LMS/EdTech applications. OneRoster 1.2 supports REST API and CSV modes. Three service areas: Rostering, Gradebook, Resource. [T06-S004]
- **context**: Replaces manual CSV exports and proprietary rostering integrations. With OneRoster, when a teacher adds a student in the SIS, it auto-provisions that student in the LMS within minutes. Used in K-12 primarily (US districts). Ed-Fi is a complementary standard for longitudinal analytics data.
- **outsider-tell**: Treating OneRoster and Ed-Fi as competitors — they serve different layers. OneRoster = operational rostering (who is in which class today); Ed-Fi = longitudinal data warehouse (multi-year student outcomes). Both can coexist.
- **related**: SIS, LMS, rostering, provisioning, Ed-Fi, FERPA

---

#### T1-12: FERPA

- **type**: Regulation
- **zh**: 家庭教育权利和隐私法
- **jurisdiction**: 美国联邦
- **issued**: 1974-08-21
- **last_revised**: 2012 (amended for cloud/EdTech vendors)
- **definition**: U.S. federal law protecting the privacy of student education records. Gives parents (and students over 18) rights to inspect, correct, and control disclosure of education records. Schools receiving federal funding must comply. [T06-S010]
- **context**: 2012 amendment introduced "school official" exception allowing EdTech vendors to access student data without consent if under a legitimate educational interest agreement with the school. This is the primary legal basis for EdTech data processing. Key concept: FERPA protects "education records," not all data about a student.
- **outsider-tell**: Saying "FERPA protects all student data" — FERPA specifically protects education records maintained by the school. A student's social media activity is not covered. Also: thinking FERPA penalties are fines — FERPA enforcement is withdrawal of federal funding (nuclear option almost never used), not per-violation fines like GDPR.
- **related**: COPPA, GDPR, PIPL, SOC 2, multi-tenancy

---

#### T1-13: COPPA

- **type**: Regulation
- **zh**: 儿童在线隐私保护法
- **jurisdiction**: 美国联邦
- **issued**: 1998 (effective 2000-04)
- **last_revised**: 2013 (FTC rule update)
- **definition**: U.S. federal law restricting online collection of personal information from children under 13. Requires verifiable parental consent before collecting data. FTC enforces; fines up to $53,088 per violation. [T06-S011]
- **context**: EdTech school exception: schools can provide consent on behalf of parents for educational purposes within school context. This is the primary compliance path for K-12 EdTech. Key data types covered: persistent identifiers, geolocation, photos/audio/video of children.
- **outsider-tell**: Thinking the age cutoff is 16 (that's GDPR for some member states). COPPA = under 13. Also: assuming the school consent exception covers unlimited data use — it only covers use within the educational context; EdTech vendors cannot use school-collected COPPA data for behavioral advertising.
- **related**: FERPA, GDPR, PIPL, WCAG

#### T1-14: PLG (Product-Led Growth)

- **type**: Acronym/Strategy
- **zh**: 产品驱动增长
- **definition**: A go-to-market strategy where the product itself is the primary driver of user acquisition, conversion, and expansion — via freemium, free trial, or viral sharing. Contrasts with SLG (Sales-Led Growth) where a sales team drives deals. [T04-S024]
- **context**: In EdTech, PLG manifests as: teacher-adopted tools that spread within a school (Kahoot, Google Classroom), freemium LMS (Canvas Free for Teachers), or students self-enrolling in MOOC platforms (Coursera). The risk in education: institutional procurement gates (IT approval, FERPA review) can block bottom-up PLG spread. PLG-to-SLG motion ("land with teacher, expand to district") is the dominant EdTech GTM pattern.
- **outsider-tell**: Thinking PLG and freemium are the same thing. PLG is a strategy; freemium is one pricing tactic that enables PLG. Also: believing PLG works equally well in K-12 district procurement contexts — district IT departments and FERPA compliance requirements typically require top-down vendor approval even if teachers love the product.
- **related**: SLG, CAC, NRR, ARR

#### T1-15: CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)

- **type**: Acronym/SaaS Metric
- **zh**: 客户获取成本
- **definition**: Total sales and marketing spend divided by number of new customers acquired in the same period. CAC Payback Period = CAC / (ARPU × Gross Margin) — time to recover acquisition cost. [T04-S003]
- **context**: EdTech-specific CAC dynamics: district sales cycles are 6-18 months, driving high CAC. Charter school and private school sales are faster (3-6 months). MOOC platforms have low CAC for individual learners (content marketing) but high institutional CAC. Benchmark: CAC payback <12 months for fast-growth, <24 months acceptable in enterprise EdTech.
- **outsider-tell**: Forgetting to include fully-loaded sales team cost (salaries, commissions, tools, overhead) in CAC calculation — using only marketing spend inflates the metric. Also: not segmenting CAC by customer segment (individual teacher vs district vs higher ed institution).
- **related**: ARR, LTV, NRR, PLG

---

#### T1-16: MOOC (Massive Open Online Course)

- **type**: Acronym/Term
- **zh**: 大规模在线开放课程
- **definition**: Online courses designed for unlimited participation and open access. Typically include video lectures, auto-graded assessments, discussion forums, and certificates. Completion rates: typically 5-15% of enrollees. [T06-S014]
- **context**: Two pedagogical models: xMOOC (instructor-led, structured content, closed licensing — Coursera/edX model) vs cMOOC (connectivist, learner-generated, open — Stephen Downes/George Siemens model). SPOC (Small Private Online Course) = MOOC structure with enrollment cap, used by universities for blended learning. Major platforms: Coursera, edX (2U), Udacity, FutureLearn.
- **outsider-tell**: Treating low completion rates (~10%) as a failure metric — MOOC platforms define "intent" differently; a user who watches 2 videos and gets what they need has completed their intent. Also: confusing xMOOC and cMOOC — in practice "MOOC" almost always refers to xMOOC.
- **related**: LMS, LXP, SPOC, OER, completion rate

---

#### T1-17: OER (Open Educational Resources)

- **type**: Acronym/Term
- **zh**: 开放教育资源
- **definition**: Teaching, learning, and research materials released under open licenses (typically Creative Commons) that allow free use, modification, and redistribution. The 5R framework: Retain, Reuse, Revise, Remix, Redistribute. [T06-S015]
- **context**: Major OER repositories: OpenStax (peer-reviewed textbooks), MIT OpenCourseWare (pioneer since 2002), OER Commons, MERLOT. OER reduces textbook cost barriers. EdTech SaaS business model tension: OER democratizes content but reduces publisher integration revenue. Some vendors (Lumen Learning) built businesses on OER curation + LMS integration.
- **outsider-tell**: Thinking OER = free and therefore low quality. Many OER (OpenStax textbooks) are peer-reviewed by subject experts. The quality signal is peer review, not price. Also: confusing "open source" (code license) with "open educational resource" (content license) — different licensing frameworks.
- **related**: LMS, digital courseware, publisher integration, Creative Commons

---

#### T1-18: SSO (Single Sign-On)

- **type**: Acronym/Technology
- **zh**: 单点登录
- **definition**: Authentication mechanism allowing users to log in once and access multiple applications. In EdTech, enables students and teachers to access LMS, SIS, third-party tools (LTI), and library systems with one institutional login. [T06-S024]
- **context**: Three main protocols in EdTech: (1) SAML 2.0 — XML-based, widely used in higher ed (Shibboleth consortium); (2) OAuth 2.0 — token-based authorization framework (Google Workspace for Education uses this); (3) OIDC (OpenID Connect) — identity layer on OAuth 2.0, newer, more LXP-friendly. Most EdTech enterprise sales require SAML 2.0 support. "Google Sign-In" and "Clever SSO" are common K-12 SSO implementations.
- **outsider-tell**: Treating OAuth and SSO as synonyms — OAuth is an authorization protocol, not inherently an authentication/SSO protocol. OIDC adds authentication on top of OAuth. True SSO requires an identity provider (IdP) like Okta, Azure AD, or Google.
- **related**: SAML, OIDC, LTI, multi-tenancy, FERPA

---

#### T1-19: 双减政策

- **type**: Regulation
- **zh**: 双减政策（减轻义务教育阶段学生作业负担和校外培训负担）
- **jurisdiction**: 中国
- **issued**: 2021-07-24
- **last_revised**: -- (ongoing enforcement)
- **definition**: Chinese government policy (2021) prohibiting for-profit tutoring in core academic subjects (语文、数学、英语等) for K9 students during weekends, holidays, and school vacations. Banned new licenses for academic tutoring companies; forced existing companies to convert to non-profit. [T06-S022]
- **context**: Decimated the $100B+ Chinese K12 tutoring industry (TAL, New Oriental, Yuanfudao). Forced EdTech pivot to: vocational education (职业教育), adult learning, non-academic subjects (art, sports, programming), and B2B school software (校内信息化). SaaS platforms serving school administration (not tutoring) were largely unaffected or benefited from increased school-side digitization budgets.
- **outsider-tell**: Thinking 双减 banned all EdTech in China — it banned for-profit academic tutoring for K9, not EdTech in general. School-facing SaaS (教务管理、智慧校园) grew post-双减.
- **related**: 素质教育, 教育信息化2.0, PIPL, K12

---

#### T1-20: Adaptive learning

- **type**: Term
- **zh**: 自适应学习
- **definition**: Technology-mediated learning that dynamically adjusts content difficulty, sequence, and pacing based on real-time learner performance data using algorithms and AI. Built on Computerized Adaptive Testing (CAT) methodology. [T06-S020]
- **context**: 86% of recent US studies report positive learning outcomes from adaptive learning. Three system components: expert model (content), student model (performance tracking), instructional model (delivery engine). Major EdTech adaptive platforms: Knewton (acquired by Wiley), ALEKS (McGraw-Hill), Smart Sparrow, DreamBox (math K-8). Related to but distinct from personalized learning (broader concept including teacher-directed customization).
- **outsider-tell**: Equating "adaptive" with "AI-powered" — early adaptive systems used rule-based decision trees, not ML. True AI-driven adaptive learning uses NLP and predictive modeling. Also: confusing adaptive learning with differentiated instruction — differentiated = teacher adjusts; adaptive = system adjusts automatically.
- **related**: LXP, Bloom's 2-sigma problem, mastery learning, CAT

---

#### T1-21: Rostering

- **type**: Term
- **zh**: 名册管理 / 自动配班
- **definition**: The process of creating and maintaining class lists (rosters) that define which students belong to which teacher's class in which course. In EdTech SaaS, automated rostering uses APIs (OneRoster, Clever, ClassLink) to sync student-teacher-class assignments from SIS to LMS and all connected tools. [T06-S004]
- **context**: Historically, rostering was done by CSV upload at semester start — error-prone and manual. Automated rostering via OneRoster or Clever means when a student changes schools or a teacher's class changes in the SIS, all connected EdTech tools update within minutes. "Rostering problem" is the #1 implementation pain point cited by K-12 EdTech buyers.
- **outsider-tell**: Underestimating rostering complexity — a mid-size district may have 50+ EdTech tools each needing up-to-date rosters, with students who transfer between schools, teachers with multiple classes, and co-teachers. "We'll just do CSV exports" is a classic underprepared-buyer statement.
- **related**: OneRoster, SIS, provisioning, Clever, Ed-Fi

---

#### T1-22: Grade passback

- **type**: Term
- **zh**: 成绩回传
- **definition**: The mechanism by which a third-party tool (external quiz, publisher content, simulation) returns a student's score back to the LMS gradebook automatically, without teacher manual re-entry. Enabled by LTI Assignment and Grade Services (AGS) in LTI 1.3 Advantage. [T06-S003]
- **context**: Without grade passback, teachers must manually transcribe scores from 5+ tools into the LMS — a major time cost. With LTI Advantage AGS, scores appear in the LMS gradebook in real time. Critical feature for publisher integrations and assessment platforms selling to higher ed and K-12. Also called "grade sync" or "gradebook sync."
- **outsider-tell**: Assuming grade passback is a simple API call — it requires the tool to be registered as an LTI 1.3 tool, the LMS to support AGS, and the course to have a linked assignment. The chain of configuration is where implementations break.
- **related**: LTI, LMS, OneRoster gradebook, SIS

---

#### T1-23: LRS (Learning Record Store)

- **type**: Acronym
- **zh**: 学习记录存储库
- **definition**: A database specifically designed to store xAPI statements (actor-verb-object learning events). Can be embedded within an LMS or deployed as a standalone service. The LRS is to xAPI what the LMS is to SCORM — the system that receives and stores learning data. [T06-S002]
- **context**: Standalone LRS vendors: Watershed LRS, Learning Locker (open source), SCORM Cloud LRS (Rustici). Institutional use case: aggregate xAPI data from mobile apps, simulations, virtual labs, and LMS into one LRS for unified learning analytics. Corporate L&D uses LRS + BI tools (Tableau, Power BI) for learning analytics dashboards.
- **outsider-tell**: Thinking every LMS has an LRS built in — most traditional LMSs (Blackboard, older Canvas versions) have partial xAPI support but not a full LRS. A proper LRS stores raw statements queryable by any BI tool; an LMS's xAPI support often only stores completion data.
- **related**: xAPI, cmi5, LMS, Caliper Analytics

---

#### T1-24: Data isolation

- **type**: Term
- **zh**: 数据隔离
- **definition**: The guarantee that one tenant's data in a multi-tenant SaaS system cannot be accessed, queried, or leaked to another tenant. Achieved through row-level security (shared DB), separate schemas, or separate databases. [T06-S018]
- **context**: In EdTech, data isolation is not just a security requirement — it's a regulatory requirement under FERPA (U.S.) and PIPL (China). District and university procurement checklist invariably includes: "How is our student data isolated from other institutions?" SOC 2 Type II audit validates isolation controls. Penetration testing specifically targets cross-tenant data leakage.
- **outsider-tell**: Claiming "full data isolation" when using a pooled-database architecture with only application-layer row filtering — this is logical isolation, not physical isolation. Sophisticated buyers (universities with InfoSec teams) will ask whether isolation is enforced at the database level, not just application level.
- **related**: multi-tenancy, FERPA, SOC 2, PIPL

---

#### T1-25: Completion rate

- **type**: Term
- **zh**: 完课率
- **definition**: Percentage of enrolled learners who complete a defined learning unit (module, course, program). In MOOCs, typically 5-15%. In corporate compliance training, expected 80-95%+. In K-12 online courses, varies 60-80%. Completion rate is heavily context-dependent. [T04-S006]
- **context**: MOOC completion paradox: raw completion rate (~10%) looks terrible but many learners enroll with limited intent (just watching 2 videos). Intent-adjusted completion (completion among learners who intended to complete) is much higher. EdTech vendors should be careful about which denominator they use (enrolled vs active vs stated-intent).
- **outsider-tell**: Using raw enrollment as the denominator for MOOC completion and calling ~10% a failure — the EDUCAUSE/HarvardX research shows completion among intent-to-complete users is 40%+. Benchmark depends entirely on course type, delivery mode, and learner cohort.
- **related**: MOOC, LMS, xAPI tracking, adaptive learning

### Tier 2 详细条目 (精简版)

---

#### T2-01: MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)

- **type**: Acronym/Metric | **zh**: 月经常性收入
- **definition**: Monthly normalized recurring subscription revenue. MRR = ARR / 12 for annual contracts. Key for early-stage SaaS where annual contract sizes are small. [T04-S003]
- **outsider-tell**: Mixing annual contract payments with MRR — a customer who pays $12,000/year upfront contributes $1,000 MRR, not $12,000.
- **related**: ARR, NRR, churn

---

#### T2-02: ARPU (Average Revenue Per User)

- **type**: Acronym/Metric | **zh**: 每用户平均收入
- **definition**: ARR (or MRR) divided by active user or customer count. In EdTech, "user" can mean individual teacher, student seat, or institution — always specify the unit. [T04-S003]
- **related**: ARR, seat expansion, LTV

---

#### T2-03: LTV (Lifetime Value)

- **type**: Acronym/Metric | **zh**: 客户生命周期价值
- **definition**: Projected total revenue from a customer over their relationship lifetime. LTV = ARPU × Gross Margin / Churn Rate. LTV:CAC ratio >3x is the typical SaaS benchmark. [T04-S003]
- **related**: CAC, ARR, NRR, churn

---

#### T2-04: Churn rate

- **type**: Term | **zh**: 流失率
- **definition**: Percentage of customers (logo churn) or revenue (revenue churn) lost in a period. In EdTech, distinguish: voluntary churn (switched vendors), involuntary churn (budget cut, school closure), and policy churn (regulation change). [T04-S003]
- **related**: NRR, logo retention, ARR

---

#### T2-05: Logo retention

- **type**: Term | **zh**: 客户数留存率
- **definition**: Percentage of customer accounts (logos) that renew. Distinct from NRR which is dollar-based. A company can have 95% logo retention but 90% NRR if retained customers downgrade seats. [T04-S003]
- **related**: NRR, churn rate, ARR

---

#### T2-06: Seat expansion

- **type**: Term | **zh**: 席位扩张
- **definition**: Growth in ARR from existing customers adding more user licenses. Primary driver of NRR >100% in EdTech SaaS. Expansion triggers: enrollment growth, new department adoption, district-wide rollout after pilot. [T04-S003]
- **related**: NRR, ARR, land-and-expand

---

#### T2-07: SLG (Sales-Led Growth)

- **type**: Acronym | **zh**: 销售驱动增长
- **definition**: GTM strategy where sales team drives new customer acquisition, typically through outbound prospecting, RFP responses, and relationship-based selling. Dominant model in enterprise K-12 district and higher ed institutional sales. [T04-S030]
- **outsider-tell**: Treating PLG and SLG as mutually exclusive — most mature EdTech companies run PLG (teacher adoption) at the bottom and SLG (district contract) at the top simultaneously.
- **related**: PLG, CAC, ARR

---

#### T2-08: cmi5

- **type**: Standard | **zh**: cmi5
- **issued**: 2016 | **last_revised**: 2020
- **definition**: An xAPI profile specification that governs how LMS-launched content should use xAPI statements. Fills the gap SCORM used to fill: how content is launched, how completion/pass/fail is reported, how session data is structured — but using xAPI's LRS-based architecture instead of SCORM's LMS-bound API. [T06-S002]
- **context**: Often described as "the best of SCORM + the best of xAPI." ADL maintains it. Adoption growing but still behind SCORM 1.2 in legacy content libraries.
- **related**: xAPI, SCORM, LRS, LMS

---

#### T2-09: Caliper Analytics

- **type**: Standard | **zh**: 学习行为分析标准
- **issued**: 2015 | **last_revised**: 2021 (v1.2)
- **definition**: 1EdTech standard that provides a common language for capturing learning activity data (16 metric profiles covering annotation, assessment, grading, media, reading). Sends data to a Sensor API endpoint. Complementary to xAPI — both track learning events but with different data models. [T06-S005]
- **context**: Primarily adopted in higher education LMS integrations. Canvas, Blackboard, and D2L implement Caliper sensors. Not as widely deployed as xAPI globally.
- **related**: xAPI, LRS, 1EdTech, LMS

---

#### T2-10: QTI (Question and Test Interoperability)

- **type**: Acronym/Standard | **zh**: 题目与测试互操作标准
- **issued**: 1999 | **last_revised**: 2022 (QTI 3.0)
- **definition**: 1EdTech standard for packaging and exchanging assessment items (questions, tests) between authoring tools, item banks, and delivery platforms. QTI 3.0 (2022) merges QTI and APIP (Accessible Portable Item Protocol) with improved accessibility (WCAG 2.1 AA, Section 508). [T06-S006]
- **context**: Critical for publishers and assessment vendors: without QTI, assessment content is locked to one platform. With QTI, a district can switch testing platforms without losing their item bank. Supports CAT (Computer Adaptive Testing).
- **related**: LTI, WCAG, LMS, assessment, Caliper

---

#### T2-11: Open Badges

- **type**: Standard | **zh**: 开放徽章
- **issued**: 2011 (Mozilla) | **last_revised**: 2023 (Open Badges 3.0, 1EdTech)
- **definition**: A standard for digital credentials (badges) that contain verifiable metadata: who issued the badge, who earned it, what criteria were met, and when. Open Badges 3.0 uses W3C Verifiable Credentials for cryptographic signing. [T06-S007]
- **context**: Originally developed by Mozilla (2011), transferred to 1EdTech. Used for micro-credentials, professional development, and co-curricular achievements. Can be aggregated into CLR (Comprehensive Learner Record). Adopted by LinkedIn, Credly, Acclaim for employer-visible credentials.
- **related**: CLR, micro-credentials, stackable credentials, LMS

---

#### T2-12: CLR (Comprehensive Learner Record)

- **type**: Acronym/Standard | **zh**: 综合学习档案
- **issued**: 2020 | **last_revised**: 2023 (CLR 2.0)
- **definition**: A 1EdTech standard for bundling multiple achievement credentials (courses, competencies, skills, Open Badges) into a single verifiable learner record. CLR 2.0 uses W3C Verifiable Credentials and can include credentials from multiple institutions. [T06-S008]
- **context**: Designed to replace or supplement transcripts as a richer record of what a learner knows and can do. Early adoption in competency-based education programs. Connected to stackable credentials and lifelong learning ecosystem.
- **related**: Open Badges, stackable credentials, micro-credentials, OBE

---

#### T2-13: WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines)

- **type**: Acronym/Standard | **zh**: Web内容无障碍指南
- **issued**: 1999 (WCAG 1.0) | **last_revised**: 2023 (WCAG 2.2); WCAG 3.0 in draft
- **definition**: W3C standard defining how to make web content accessible to people with disabilities. Three conformance levels: A (basic), AA (standard requirement), AAA (advanced). EdTech typically targets WCAG 2.1 AA or 2.2 AA. Four principles: Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, Robust (POUR). [T06-S009]
- **context**: Section 508 (US federal accessibility law) references WCAG 2.0 AA. ADA compliance for education increasingly maps to WCAG 2.1 AA. QTI 3.0 requires WCAG 2.1 AA compliance. European Accessibility Act (2025) mandates WCAG 2.1 AA for digital products in EU.
- **outsider-tell**: Saying "we're WCAG compliant" without specifying version and level — the meaningful claim is "WCAG 2.1 AA" or "WCAG 2.2 AA." "WCAG compliant" at Level A is nearly meaningless.
- **related**: Section 508, QTI, 1EdTech Certification, accessibility

---

#### T2-14: Ed-Fi

- **type**: Standard | **zh**: Ed-Fi K-12数据标准
- **issued**: 2009 | **last_revised**: ongoing
- **definition**: An open data standard and REST API specification for K-12 education data, maintained by the Ed-Fi Alliance (nonprofit, funded by Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation). Focuses on longitudinal, cross-system analytics — student outcomes over time across district systems. [T06-S025]
- **context**: Ed-Fi ODS/API is a reference implementation for state education agencies and large districts. Complementary to OneRoster: OneRoster handles operational rostering; Ed-Fi handles analytical data warehouse. Ed-Fi Alliance and 1EdTech have collaborated on OneRoster support in Ed-Fi ODS.
- **outsider-tell**: Treating Ed-Fi as an alternative to OneRoster — they serve different layers. OneRoster = real-time operational data exchange; Ed-Fi = longitudinal data standards and analytics.
- **related**: OneRoster, SIS, LMS, Caliper

---

#### T2-15: GDPR

- **type**: Regulation | **zh**: 欧盟通用数据保护条例
- **jurisdiction**: 欧盟
- **issued**: 2018-05-25
- **last_revised**: -- (ongoing guidance updates)
- **definition**: EU regulation governing personal data collection, processing, and transfer. Key obligations for EdTech: lawful basis for processing (consent or legitimate interest), data subject rights (access, erasure, portability), data protection by design, DPO appointment, breach notification within 72 hours.
- **context**: GDPR applies to any EdTech vendor serving EU students regardless of where the vendor is based. Special categories of data (health, biometrics) require explicit consent. Cross-border data transfer to non-EU countries (including US) requires SCCs (Standard Contractual Clauses) or adequacy decision.
- **outsider-tell**: Thinking GDPR only applies to European companies — any vendor processing EU student data must comply. Also: assuming "consent" is always the right legal basis — in employment/education contexts, consent is often not appropriate because of power imbalance; legitimate interest or contractual necessity may be more appropriate.
- **related**: FERPA, COPPA, PIPL, data isolation, SOC 2

---

#### T2-16: 个保法 (PIPL)

- **type**: Regulation | **zh**: 个人信息保护法
- **jurisdiction**: 中国
- **issued**: 2021-11-01
- **last_revised**: --
- **definition**: China's comprehensive personal information protection law, analogous to GDPR. Key obligations: purpose limitation, data minimization, consent requirements (separate consent for sensitive data), cross-border transfer restrictions (security assessment required for large transfers), localization requirements.
- **context**: EdTech vendors in China must comply with PIPL + Cybersecurity Law + Data Security Law (the "三法" framework). Student data qualifies as sensitive personal information under PIPL (minor data). Platforms must implement 等保 2.0 (cybersecurity level protection) as an operational requirement.
- **outsider-tell**: Treating PIPL as identical to GDPR — while structurally similar, PIPL has stricter cross-border transfer requirements and Chinese regulators have different enforcement priorities. Data localization (storing data within China) is a de facto requirement for education platforms serving Chinese students.
- **related**: 双减政策, 等保2.0, GDPR, FERPA, data isolation

---

#### T2-17: SOC 2 Type II

- **type**: Certification | **zh**: SOC 2 二型安全审计
- **issuer**: AICPA (American Institute of Certified Public Accountants)
- **definition**: A security audit standard for SaaS vendors assessing controls across five Trust Service Criteria: Security, Availability, Processing Integrity, Confidentiality, and Privacy. Type I = controls designed correctly at a point in time. Type II = controls operating effectively over 6-12 months. EdTech buyers require Type II.
- **context**: SOC 2 Type II is the de facto security credential for EdTech vendors selling to US schools. District and university procurement teams ask for it by name. Without it, enterprise sales stalls. Common scope: Security + Availability (most EdTech); add Confidentiality for sensitive student data; add Privacy for FERPA-aligned platforms.
- **outsider-tell**: Presenting SOC 2 Type I as equivalent to Type II in enterprise sales — sophisticated buyers know Type I only proves controls were designed; Type II proves they actually worked. "We're SOC 2 certified" without specifying Type II is a yellow flag.
- **related**: ISO 27001, FERPA, multi-tenancy, data isolation

---

#### T2-18: ISO 27001

- **type**: Certification | **zh**: 信息安全管理体系
- **issuer**: ISO (International Organization for Standardization)
- **definition**: International standard for information security management systems (ISMS). Requires organizations to systematically examine security risks and implement controls. Certification requires third-party audit and annual surveillance. More process-focused than SOC 2's technical controls focus.
- **context**: More commonly required in EU and Asia-Pacific EdTech sales than US (where SOC 2 Type II dominates). For EdTech vendors targeting global markets (EU universities, Asia-Pacific schools), ISO 27001 may be required alongside or instead of SOC 2.
- **related**: SOC 2 Type II, 等保2.0, GDPR, FERPA

---

#### T2-19: 等保 2.0

- **type**: Certification/Regulation | **zh**: 网络安全等级保护2.0
- **issuer**: 中国公安部
- **definition**: China's mandatory cybersecurity level protection standard, updated in 2019 (等保2.0). Five levels; EdTech platforms typically require Level 2 (one-year review cycle) or Level 3 (annual review + real-time monitoring) depending on data sensitivity and user scale.
- **context**: Required for any EdTech platform operating in China with significant user base. Level 2 = general commercial systems; Level 3 = systems important to national economy or involving sensitive data. Student data platforms serving large-scale users typically must achieve Level 3. Combined with PIPL and Cybersecurity Law as the "三法" compliance framework.
- **outsider-tell**: Treating 等保 as a one-time checkbox — 等保 2.0 requires ongoing compliance, not just initial certification. Level 3 requires real-time security monitoring and annual third-party assessment.
- **related**: PIPL, 双减政策, ISO 27001, data isolation

---

#### T2-20: SAML (Security Assertion Markup Language)

- **type**: Acronym | **zh**: 安全断言标记语言
- **definition**: An XML-based open standard for exchanging authentication and authorization data between an identity provider (IdP) and a service provider (SP). SAML 2.0 (2005) is the version used in EdTech. Dominant SSO protocol in higher education. [T06-S024]
- **context**: Higher ed IdP ecosystem: Shibboleth (open source, widely used in research universities), Azure AD (Microsoft), Google Workspace for Education. SAML 2.0 support is a baseline requirement for any EdTech platform targeting higher education institutional sales. K-12 increasingly uses OIDC/OAuth via Clever or ClassLink.
- **related**: SSO, OIDC, OAuth, LTI, multi-tenancy

---

#### T2-21: OBE (Outcome-Based Education)

- **type**: Acronym | **zh**: 结果导向教育 / 成果本位教育
- **definition**: An educational framework that organizes curriculum, instruction, and assessment backward from defined learning outcomes/competencies. Students demonstrate mastery of outcomes rather than complete seat time. Foundational to competency-based education (CBE) and mastery learning. [T06-S016]
- **context**: Increasingly adopted in vocational education, professional licensing programs, and higher ed accreditation frameworks. EdTech SaaS connects OBE to technology: outcomes mapped to content items, assessments aligned to outcomes, analytics dashboards showing outcome achievement rates. CLR and Open Badges provide the credentialing layer for OBE outcomes.
- **related**: mastery learning, CLR, Open Badges, competency-based education, Bloom's taxonomy

---

#### T2-22: PLA (Prior Learning Assessment)

- **type**: Acronym | **zh**: 先前学习评估
- **definition**: A process for evaluating and formally recognizing knowledge and skills acquired outside formal education (work experience, military service, self-study). Methods: portfolio review, challenge exams, skills demonstrations. Can translate to college credit or credential recognition. [T06-S019]
- **context**: Growing importance in US higher ed as institutions compete for adult learners. EdTech SaaS opportunity: platforms that automate PLA workflows (evidence collection, expert review, credit recommendation) reduce administrative burden. Connected to micro-credentials and stackable credentials movement.
- **related**: micro-credentials, OBE, CLR, stackable credentials, competency-based education

---

#### T2-23: Micro-credential

- **type**: Term | **zh**: 微证书
- **definition**: A short, focused credential demonstrating competency in a specific skill or subject area, typically shorter than a full degree or certificate program. Can be represented as Open Badges or CLR components. Stackable toward larger credentials.
- **context**: Driven by workforce demand for skills-based hiring. Major platforms: Coursera Certificates, LinkedIn Learning Certificates, edX MicroMasters. Corporate buyers increasingly accept micro-credentials alongside degrees for job qualifications.
- **related**: stackable credentials, Open Badges, CLR, OBE, PLA

---

#### T2-24: Stackable credentials

- **type**: Term | **zh**: 可叠加证书
- **definition**: A sequence of credentials where each builds on the previous, and the collection of lower credentials can stack toward a higher qualification (e.g., certificate → associate degree → bachelor's degree). Enabled by CLR and Open Badges technology.
- **context**: Policy driver: US Department of Labor promotes stackable credentials for workforce development. EdTech opportunity: platforms that visualize and track credential stacking pathways and connect to employer recognition.
- **related**: micro-credentials, CLR, Open Badges, PLA, OBE

---

#### T2-25: Yield rate

- **type**: Term | **zh**: 录取接受率
- **definition**: In higher education enrollment management: the percentage of admitted students who accept the offer and enroll. Yield = (Enrolled students) / (Admitted students) × 100. High-selectivity schools (Harvard) have high yield; open-access institutions compete on yield. [T06-S017]
- **context**: EdTech CRM platforms (Slate, Salesforce Education Cloud) optimize yield through personalized outreach, virtual campus tours, financial aid modeling. Yield decline = enrollment decline = budget crisis for tuition-dependent institutions.
- **related**: enrollment funnel, melt rate, SIS, CRM

---

#### T2-26: Melt rate

- **type**: Term | **zh**: 录取流失率
- **definition**: The percentage of students who pay a deposit (confirming intent to enroll) but fail to show up on the first day of classes. Melt typically occurs over summer between deposit deadline (May 1) and fall enrollment. Causes: found a better offer, financial issues, life events. [T06-S017]
- **context**: Summer melt is a persistent problem in US higher ed. EdTech interventions: automated summer check-in systems, financial aid counseling apps (TheDream.US), orientation readiness platforms. Melt rate of 10-20% is common at open-access institutions.
- **related**: yield rate, enrollment funnel, SIS

---

#### T2-27: Provisioning

- **type**: Term | **zh**: 账号自动配置
- **definition**: The automated process of creating, updating, and deprovisioning user accounts and access rights across EdTech applications when student/teacher records change in the SIS. Triggered by OneRoster API events or directory sync (SCIM protocol).
- **context**: "Deprovisioning" (removing access when a student graduates or leaves) is as important as provisioning for FERPA compliance — former students should lose access to institutional systems. SCIM (System for Cross-domain Identity Management) is the emerging standard for provisioning automation.
- **related**: rostering, OneRoster, SSO, FERPA, SIS

---

#### T2-28: xMOOC vs cMOOC

- **type**: Term | **zh**: 扩展型vs联通型MOOC
- **definition**: Two pedagogical models of MOOCs. xMOOC: instructor-led, structured content, video lectures + auto-graded assessments, scale-first design (Coursera, edX, Udacity). cMOOC: connectivist model, learner-generated knowledge, networked learning, emerged from George Siemens/Stephen Downes connectivism theory (CCK08, 2008). [T06-S014]
- **context**: In practice, "MOOC" almost exclusively refers to xMOOC in commercial/institutional contexts. cMOOC is primarily an academic research construct. Understanding the distinction signals fluency with EdTech pedagogy history.
- **related**: MOOC, SPOC, LMS, OER, completion rate

---

#### T2-29: SPOC (Small Private Online Course)

- **type**: Acronym | **zh**: 小型私密在线课程
- **definition**: A MOOC-format course offered to a limited, curated enrollment (typically 10-500 students) within an institution or a defined cohort. Combines online learning scalability with the community of a traditional class. [T06-S014]
- **context**: Used by universities for blended learning (flipped classroom): students watch SPOC videos at home, come to campus for active learning. Also used by companies for internal training using external MOOC platform infrastructure.
- **related**: MOOC, flipped classroom, LMS, blended learning

---

#### T2-30: 素质教育 (Quality-Oriented Education)

- **type**: Term | **zh**: 素质教育
- **definition**: Chinese education policy concept emphasizing holistic development (creativity, critical thinking, physical health, aesthetics, civic values) over exam-focused learning (应试教育). Official policy since 1993. 双减政策 is its enforcement mechanism in the K12 private tutoring sector.
- **context**: EdTech SaaS pivot post-双减: platforms that serve 素质教育 (art, sports, programming, STEM enrichment) were exempt from 双减 restrictions. 素质教育 apps for school use received government support. Understanding this distinction separates market winners from losers in post-2021 Chinese EdTech.
- **related**: 双减政策, 教育信息化2.0, K12, OBE
