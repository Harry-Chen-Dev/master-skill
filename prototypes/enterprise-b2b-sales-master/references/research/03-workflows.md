## 各 Workflow 卡片

### 1. Run a complete discovery call（跑一个完整的需求挖掘电话）

- **One-liner**: Take a booked-but-unqualified meeting and leave with a *diagnosed, quantified business problem* + a locked next step — the single highest-leverage rep skill, and the one most often skipped in favor of a product pitch. (evidence: [T03-S001, T03-S003])
- **Trigger**: A qualified meeting is on the calendar (inbound demo request, outbound sequence reply, or referral). (evidence: [T03-S001])
- **Output**: (1) a problem articulated in the *buyer's* words with a quantified business/financial impact, (2) at least one stakeholder/political signal, (3) a suggested, dated next step the buyer agrees to. (evidence: [T03-S001])
- **入门 SOP（minimum viable steps）**:
  1. **Set an agenda / up-front contract (PPO)** — open by agreeing Purpose / Plan / Outcome: "the goal is to see if we *can* help or not… by the end let's have a frank discussion on fit." Sets the expectation this is not a product dump and earns permission to ask. (who: rep; output: buyer buy-in, no "just show me a demo" interruption). (evidence: [T03-S001, T03-S004])
     - **跳过后果**: with no up-front contract the buyer expects a demo; the rep gets interrupted ("can I just see it?"), pitches blind, and never surfaces the real problem → the call becomes a feature tour. (evidence: [T03-S001])
  2. **Find the situation** — "I'm sure you don't take every cold call — what prompted you to take this one?" Surfaces why *now*. (evidence: [T03-S001])
     - **跳过后果**: rep solves a problem the buyer doesn't have / can't tie to a trigger event → no urgency, deal stalls in "no decision." (evidence: [T03-S032])
  3. **Turn situation into a problem** — use "typically" framing ("typically when someone's in your spot it's because A or B — which is it?") + Sandler pain-funnel nesting (vague → specific → consequence). (evidence: [T03-S001, T03-S003])
     - **跳过后果**: a surface symptom, not a root problem; the value story later has nothing to attach to. (evidence: [T03-S003])
  4. **Quantify the impact** — "magic moment" / implication questions ("when did you realize this was a problem? what's it costing?") drive vague frustration to a dollar figure the buyer says out loud. (evidence: [T03-S001, T03-S003])
     - **跳过后果**: without quantified cost-of-problem there is no business case and no defense against "no decision" / a cheaper competitor. (evidence: [T03-S003, T03-S032])
  5. **Lock the next step (the "5-minute drill")** — pressure-test intent ("is it worth continuing to invest time together?"), get a timeline ("latest you'd want this in place?"), and *suggest* (don't ask) the next-next step aligned to how they buy. Only advance deals with genuine near-term (≈6-month) intent. (evidence: [T03-S001])
     - **跳过后果**: "great call!" with no dated next step = a deal that quietly dies; the rep can't forecast it and chases a ghost for weeks. (evidence: [T03-S001])
- **资深路径（差异点）**:
  - **skip**: seniors **skip the demo/pitch entirely** on call 1 — they hold the product back until business pain is evidenced; juniors pitch features to fill silence. (evidence: [T03-S014, T03-S001])
  - **optimize**: seniors **optimize impact quantification** — they don't accept the first symptom; they stack implication questions until the *buyer* states the cost, and they hit the golden ~43/57 talk-to-listen ratio (talk less, ask more). Juniors talk too much and accept vague pain. (evidence: [T03-S031, T03-S003])
  - **add**: seniors **add pre-call research** (a discovery prep sheet: trigger events, org chart, likely metrics) *and* **a multithread-ask** ("who else is affected? should they be in the next conversation?") — turning one contact into a committee map mid-call. Juniors walk in cold and stay single-threaded. (evidence: [T03-S002, T03-S028])
- **近期变化（近 12 个月）**: 2024-2026, AI conversation-intelligence (Gong-class) now auto-scores discovery calls (talk-ratio, did-you-quantify-pain, multithreading) and auto-fills CRM/MEDDIC fields, so *coaching* the discovery motion is faster — but the **core question architecture (situation→problem→implication→quantify→lock) is decades-stable (SPIN 1988, Sandler 1967)**. The change is the coaching/measurement layer, not the motion. 触发事件: conversation-intelligence + AI note-takers maturing. 采用率: conversation intelligence is table-stakes for teams >~20 reps (caveat 业内观察). (evidence: [T03-S030, T03-S003])
- **典型耗时**: 入门 SOP — 30-45 min call + ~30 min prep. 资深路径 — same call length but 1-2 hr pre-call research on complex/strategic accounts; a senior gets in one call what a junior takes 2-3 calls to surface.
- **关键工具（Track 02）**: Sandler pain funnel (artifact, 必备-discovery), SPIN/SPICED question set (artifact), Gong (conversation intelligence, 必备), discovery prep sheet (artifact). (evidence: [T03-S003, T03-S035, T03-S030])
- **关键人物（Track 01）**: Neil Rackham (SPIN four-question sequence) and Nick Cegelski & Armand Farrokh (30MPC, the 4-step minimum-viable framework) both detail this workflow at length; David Sandler (pain funnel + up-front contract). (evidence: [T03-S001, T03-S004])
- **常见失败模式**:
  - **No up-front contract → the call gets hijacked into a demo**: the buyer drives, the rep pitches blind, no problem surfaces. Avoid: open every call agreeing Purpose/Plan/Outcome before any question. (evidence: [T03-S001])
  - **Accepting vague pain ("things could be more efficient") without quantifying → no business case**: later you can't justify budget or beat a cheaper competitor. Avoid: stack implication questions until the buyer states a dollar/time cost out loud. (evidence: [T03-S003])
  - **"Show up and throw up" — rep talks >50% of the call**: data shows won deals have the rep talking less (~43%). Avoid: ask, shut up, let silence work. (evidence: [T03-S031])
  - **Ending with no dated next step ("I'll send some info")**: the deal has no forward motion and can't be forecast. Avoid: run the 5-minute drill — intent, timeline, suggested next-next step. (evidence: [T03-S001])

### 2. Qualify / disqualify an opportunity with MEDDPICC（用 MEDDPICC 资格审查一个机会）

- **One-liner**: Decide *which deals are real* — score an opportunity against MEDDPICC, and either commit resources or **disqualify early** ("no deal beats a bad deal"). The discipline that keeps reps off doomed deals; its dark twin is **box-checking theater**. (evidence: [T03-S005, T03-S036])
- **Trigger**: A new opportunity is created (post-discovery), and/or any pre-forecast deal-inspection cadence. (evidence: [T03-S005])
- **Output**: A scored opportunity where the rep can *name* the Economic Buyer, the Champion, the Decision Criteria/Process, and the Paper Process — or a clean, documented disqualify that frees the rep's time. (evidence: [T03-S005, T03-S007])
- **入门 SOP（minimum viable steps）**:
  1. **Identify Pain + Metrics** — confirm a real, quantified business problem (M = the buyer's ROI number, not your feature). (evidence: [T03-S005])
     - **跳过后果**: "no problem, no sale" — without pain you're selling to indifference; the deal dies in no-decision. (evidence: [T03-S032])
  2. **Find the Economic Buyer** — name the one person who can say yes when everyone else says no (P&L authority + discretionary budget). (evidence: [T03-S007])
     - **跳过后果**: "if you can't name the EB, you haven't qualified — you're hoping"; the deal can't get budget and stalls at the champion's ceiling. (evidence: [T03-S007])
  3. **Map Decision Criteria + Decision Process** — the (often unstated) rules and the steps/people by which they'll decide (note: Decision Process = technical validation + business approval, two parts). (evidence: [T03-S008])
     - **跳过后果**: you optimize for the wrong criteria, get surprised by an approval step (legal/security/board), and slip the close date. (evidence: [T03-S008])
  4. **Secure + test a Champion** — someone with power, influence, and *vested interest* who sells for you when you're not in the room. (evidence: [T03-S006])
     - **跳过后果**: you mistake a friendly "coach" (no power) for a champion; nobody advocates internally and the deal stalls. (evidence: [T03-S006])
  5. **Map Paper Process + Competition** — the procurement/legal/security path to signature + who/what you're really competing against (often the status quo). (evidence: [T03-S005, T03-S032])
     - **跳过后果**: "verbal yes" but the deal dies in the paper process (redlines/security review) you never mapped. (evidence: [T03-S005])
- **资深路径（差异点）**:
  - **skip**: seniors **disqualify fast and skip deals with no pain or no path to the EB** — they'd rather kill a deal in week 1 than carry a zombie in pipeline; juniors chase everything to protect coverage optics. (evidence: [T03-S005, T03-S007])
  - **optimize**: seniors **test the champion** (make them *do* something — set up the EB meeting, share an internal doc) rather than assume; a champion who won't act isn't one. Juniors check the "Champion" CRM box because someone is friendly. (evidence: [T03-S006])
  - **add**: seniors treat MEDDPICC as a **thinking tool, not a CRM field set** — they fill gaps with *real* intel and use exit criteria; they add a gap-driven next action for every blank letter. Juniors fill the fields to satisfy the manager. (evidence: [T03-S005])
- **近期变化（近 12 个月）**: 2024-2026, AI note-takers (Momentum/Sybill-class, Momentum acquired by Salesforce Mar 2026) now **auto-populate MEDDIC fields from call transcripts** — fixing the data-entry symptom but risking *automated* box-checking (fields filled by AI, thought about by no one). The **qualification discipline itself is 28+ years stable (PTC 1996)**. 触发事件: AI auto-CRM tools + Salesforce Agentforce. (evidence: [T03-S005])
- **典型耗时**: 入门 SOP — ongoing across the deal; initial pass ~30 min per opp. 资深路径 — a senior can disqualify in one discovery call; a junior carries an unqualified deal for weeks before a manager kills it in review.
- **关键工具（Track 02）**: MEDDPICC scorecard (artifact, 必备), CRM (必备), AI auto-CRM note-takers (新兴), SPICED canvas (artifact, lighter SaaS alternative). (evidence: [T03-S005, T03-S035])
- **关键人物（Track 01）**: John McMahon ("MEDDPICC's father," deal inspection) and Andy Whyte (MEDDICC, the codified element-by-element playbook) own this workflow; Revenue Builders podcast is the long-form home. (evidence: [T03-S005, T03-S036])
- **常见失败模式**:
  - **Box-checking theater — filling MEDDPICC CRM fields without real qualification**: a fully-populated scorecard is *not* a qualified deal. Avoid: managers inspect *quality* (can you name + prove the EB?), enforce exit criteria, and coach the thinking — not the field-completion. (evidence: [T03-S005])
  - **Mistaking a Coach for a Champion**: the friendly contact who gives intel but has no power can't move the deal. Avoid: test the champion — make them do something for you. (evidence: [T03-S006])
  - **Never confirming the Economic Buyer**: you sell to the champion's ceiling and the deal can't get budget. Avoid: if you can't name the EB, the deal is unqualified — treat that as a red flag, not a detail. (evidence: [T03-S007])
  - **Treating MEDDPICC as a full sales method**: it's *qualification only* — it tells you what to know, not how to run discovery or articulate value. Avoid: pair it with SPIN/Gap (discover) + Command of the Message (value). (evidence: [T03-S005])

### 3. Build an outbound prospecting sequence from 0（从 0 建立一个外呼开发序列）

- **One-liner**: Turn an empty territory into booked discovery calls — define ICP, build a list, run a multichannel cadence, and stay legal across geographies. The most **AI-disrupted and compliance-sensitive** workflow in the industry. (evidence: [T03-S009, T03-S010, T03-S012])
- **Trigger**: A new rep book/territory, a pipeline-coverage gap (open pipe < ~3× quota, 经验法则), or a quarter-start prospecting push. (evidence: [T03-S010])
- **Output**: A running, multichannel sequence (e.g. 10-14 touches over 30 days) against an ICP-matched list, producing booked, qualified discovery calls — and a compliant opt-out/consent posture by geography. (evidence: [T03-S009, T03-S012])
- **入门 SOP（minimum viable steps）**:
  1. **Define the ICP + personas** — firmographic fit (ICP, account-level) *and* the buying-committee personas within it (person-level). (evidence: [T03-S010])
     - **跳过后果**: you spray a non-fit list, burn your domain on uninterested prospects, and book garbage meetings that waste AE time. (evidence: [T03-S010])
  2. **Build the list + enrich** — pull ICP-matched accounts/contacts (prospecting-data tools) with verified emails/phones. (evidence: [T03-S010])
     - **跳过后果**: bad data → bounces → deliverability collapse; the whole domain's emails start hitting spam. (evidence: [T03-S009])
  3. **Design the multichannel cadence** — ~10-14 touches over 30 days, phased into problem-themes: Theme 1 (days 1-7: opener + bubble-ups), Theme 2 (days 8-14: new angle + qualification), Breakup (days 15-30: yes/no). Phase out LinkedIn by theme 2 and cold calls by theme 3 to redirect effort to fresher prospects; LinkedIn exists to *lift email replies*, not to close. (evidence: [T03-S009])
     - **跳过后果**: single-channel / too-few touches misses the prospect (avg 9-12 touches to connect); one-and-done emails convert near zero. (evidence: [T03-S010])
  4. **Write interest-based, problem-led copy** — lead with context/problem, use low-pressure "any thoughts?" CTAs instead of an immediate meeting ask (raises reply rate, lowers spam flags). (evidence: [T03-S009, T03-S011])
     - **跳过后果**: "book 15 minutes Tuesday?" cold = ignored + flagged as spam; you train the prospect to delete you. (evidence: [T03-S009])
  5. **Compliance gate by geography** — CAN-SPAM (US: no B2B exemption, working opt-out in 10 days, real address); CASL (Canada: opt-in, up to $10M/violation); PECR + UK GDPR (corporate-subscriber exception, soft opt-in only for existing customers); GDPR (EU: legitimate-interest + documented LIA); TCPA for calls/SMS. (evidence: [T03-S012, T03-S013])
     - **跳过后果**: legal exposure (CAN-SPAM ~$53,088/email; CASL up to $10M; TCPA consent suits) + permanent domain/brand damage. (evidence: [T03-S012, T03-S013])
- **资深路径（差异点）**:
  - **skip**: seniors (and modern GTM teams) **skip the spray-everyone blast** — post-2024 AI-deliverability backlash, high-volume identical cadences are exactly what kills reply rates; they run fewer, sharper touches. (evidence: [T03-S009])
  - **optimize**: seniors **optimize via signals** — trigger sequences on a job-change/funding/hiring/intent signal (signal-based GTM) instead of a static list, and personalize per persona, so relevance (not volume) does the work. (evidence: [T03-S010])
  - **add**: seniors **add deliverability + compliance discipline** as first-class steps (domain warmup, list hygiene, geo-routed consent logic, suppression of opt-outs within SLA) — juniors treat compliance as an afterthought and learn it via a burned domain or a legal letter. (evidence: [T03-S012, T03-S013])
- **近期变化（近 12 个月）**: **HIGH decay, and real.** (1) 2024-2025 **AI SDR collapse** (11x claimed non-customers as customers + counted trial ARR, CEO stepped down May 2025; Artisan's "Ava" rate-limited by LinkedIn) — fully-autonomous AI prospecting at volume crashed deliverability; the field moved to *hybrid* (AI draft + human send) and *signal-based* targeting. (2) **TCPA one-to-one consent rule** (FCC 24-24) effective 2025-01-27, then **rejected by the Fifth Circuit (2026)** → an active circuit split; cross-state cold-calling consent is in flux. 触发事件: AI-SDR backlash (2025-Q1/Q2) + TCPA legal churn (2025-2026). 采用率: ~45% of teams now run hybrid AI+human (caveat 第三方/业内估计). (evidence: [T03-S009, T03-S013])
- **典型耗时**: 入门 SOP — 1-2 weeks to stand up (ICP + list + sequence build); the sequence runs 30 days. 资深路径 — a GTM engineer builds a signal-triggered, enriched sequence in days but it's continuously tuned; ongoing.
- **关键工具（Track 02）**: Salesloft/Outreach (sales engagement, 必备), Apollo/ZoomInfo/Sales Nav (prospecting data, 必备), Clay (signal-based GTM, 场景特化), Nooks (AI parallel dialer, 场景特化), AI SDR agents (新兴, cautionary). (evidence: [T03-S009, T03-S010])
- **关键人物（Track 01）**: Aaron Ross (SDR/AE specialization + Cold Calling 2.0), Jeb Blount (Fanatical Prospecting + 7-step sequences), 30MPC (the perfect outbound sequence template), Josh Braun (low-pressure, interest-based CTAs). (evidence: [T03-S009, T03-S011, T03-S034])
- **常见失败模式**:
  - **Run a fully-autonomous AI SDR at vendor-recommended volume → deliverability collapses, domain burned**: everyone pointing the same AI at the same playbook saturated inboxes in 2025. Avoid: run hybrid (AI volume + human nuance), warm domains, keep lists clean. (evidence: [T03-S009])
  - **Assume "B2B cold email is exempt from spam law"**: CAN-SPAM has *no* B2B exemption; CASL is opt-in; CCPA now covers work-contact data. Avoid: build a geo-routed compliance gate before the first send. (evidence: [T03-S012])
  - **Too few touches / single channel → never connect**: it takes ~9-12 touches across channels to reach a prospect; one email converts near zero. Avoid: 10-14 touches over 30 days, phased multichannel. (evidence: [T03-S010, T03-S009])
  - **Immediate meeting-ask CTA → ignored + spam-flagged**: cold "book a demo" requests tank reply rates. Avoid: interest-based "any thoughts?" CTAs first; escalate only on engagement. (evidence: [T03-S009])

### 4. Manage a POC / technical validation（管理一次技术验证 / POC）

- **One-liner**: When a buyer wants proof before buying, run a *time-boxed* technical validation with **pre-agreed success criteria** — done right it's a "yes" accelerant; done wrong it becomes endless free consulting. The AE↔SE collaboration motion. (evidence: [T03-S014, T03-S015])
- **Trigger**: A qualified buyer asks for hands-on proof (POC/POV) before committing; the SE/Solutions Engineer is engaged. (evidence: [T03-S015])
- **Output**: A completed, time-boxed POC that meets explicitly pre-agreed success criteria → a documented technical "win" that the AE converts into business approval. (evidence: [T03-S014, T03-S015])
- **入门 SOP（minimum viable steps）**:
  1. **Qualify whether a POC is even warranted** — confirm pain, EB engagement, and that a POC (not a demo/reference) is the right proof. (evidence: [T03-S014])
     - **跳过后果**: you run a POC for a tire-kicker with no EB/budget → weeks of SE effort for a deal that was never real. (evidence: [T03-S014])
  2. **Define success criteria + exit criteria up front** — write down, with the buyer, exactly what "success" means (which use cases, what threshold, by when), and what happens if it's met. (evidence: [T03-S008, T03-S015])
     - **跳过后果**: with no success criteria the POC has no finish line → it becomes open-ended free consulting, drags 6-8+ weeks, and the buyer keeps adding "just one more thing." (evidence: [T03-S015])
  3. **Set mutual ownership + timeline** — name who does what on both sides, with dates (often inside the Mutual Action Plan). (evidence: [T03-S017])
     - **跳过后果**: the SE does all the work, the buyer never invests, and a one-sided POC signals weak intent. (evidence: [T03-S014])
  4. **Run the POC with the SE bridging tech↔value** — the Sales Engineer (trusted technical advisor) connects technical capabilities to the quantified business value at each step. (evidence: [T03-S014])
     - **跳过后果**: a "feature-passes" POC that proves the tech works but never ties to business value → "we don't see the value of your extra features, picked a cheaper competitor." (evidence: [T03-S014])
  5. **Review the POV with the client + document the win** — formally review results against the criteria with the buyer; capture metrics for the business case. (evidence: [T03-S015])
     - **跳过后果**: a "successful" POC nobody formally agreed succeeded → the buyer re-litigates it and the technical win doesn't convert to business approval. (evidence: [T03-S015])
- **资深路径（差异点）**:
  - **skip**: seniors **skip the POC for unqualified or no-EB deals** — they offer a reference call or a focused demo instead; juniors say yes to every POC request (it feels like progress) and drown the SE team. (evidence: [T03-S014])
  - **optimize**: seniors **make success criteria the gate** — no written, buyer-agreed criteria, no POC; and they tie every technical check to a business outcome so the POC sells value, not features. Juniors start the POC to "build momentum" and define success later (never). (evidence: [T03-S015, T03-S014])
  - **add**: seniors **add mutual ownership + a dated close path** (the POC lives inside a MAP, the EB pre-agrees that a passing POC means a "yes") *and* use AI call-coaching to review SE discovery quality. Juniors run the POC in isolation from the commercial close. (evidence: [T03-S017, T03-S030])
- **近期变化（近 12 个月）**: Largely steady (the success-criteria-first discipline is long-standing) — **most significant recent shift is AI**: (1) selling *AI products* changes POC dynamics (buyers demand proof AI works on *their* data), and (2) conversation-intelligence now coaches SE-led calls. 触发事件: AI-product proliferation + SE-coaching tooling (2024-2026). 采用率: directional, no hard number. (evidence: [T03-S014, T03-S030])
- **典型耗时**: 入门 SOP — a scoped POC runs 2-6 weeks. 资深路径 — seniors compress it (tight criteria, time-box) and decline POCs that aren't qualified, *saving* SE weeks; a poorly-run POC balloons to 6-8+ weeks of free consulting. (evidence: [T03-S015])
- **关键工具（Track 02）**: SE demo/POC environment, Mutual Action Plan (artifact, 必备), MEDDPICC Decision Process field (artifact), Gong (SE-call coaching, 必备). (evidence: [T03-S008, T03-S017, T03-S030])
- **关键人物（Track 01）**: Force Management / John Kaplan (technical-sales execution, "hold the demo until business pain is evidenced"); the SE-as-trusted-advisor thesis is a Force Management staple. (evidence: [T03-S014])
- **常见失败模式**:
  - **No success criteria set up front → POC becomes endless free consulting**: with no finish line, the buyer keeps scope-creeping and the deal drags 6-8+ weeks. Avoid: write buyer-agreed success + exit criteria *before* day 1; if the buyer won't agree to criteria, that's a disqualify signal. (evidence: [T03-S015])
  - **A one-sided POC the SE does alone**: no buyer effort = weak intent + a result the buyer didn't co-own. Avoid: mutual ownership with dates inside a MAP. (evidence: [T03-S017])
  - **Feature-passes-but-no-value POC**: tech works, but it's untied to a business outcome → lose to a cheaper competitor. Avoid: SE connects each capability to the quantified business value. (evidence: [T03-S014])
  - **Running a POC for an unqualified / no-EB deal**: weeks of scarce SE time on a deal that was never real. Avoid: qualify the POC (pain + EB + right-proof) before committing the SE. (evidence: [T03-S014])

  - [Primary] Force Management effective POC/POV (Keno Helmi, Revenue Builders): https://www.forcemanagement.com/effective-proof-of-concepts-with-keno-helmi (T03-S015)

### 5. Build & sell a business case to the economic buyer（构建业务价值论证并卖给经济决策人）

- **One-liner**: Convert a quantified problem into a value narrative (before/after + business outcomes + ROI) and deliver it *to the person who controls the budget* — the move from "feature selling to users" to "value selling to the EB." (evidence: [T03-S016, T03-S007])
- **Trigger**: Discovery has quantified the pain; the deal needs budget approval / a business case to advance to proposal. (evidence: [T03-S016])
- **Output**: A value story — Before scenarios → Negative consequences → Positive Business Outcomes (PBOs) → Required Capabilities → Differentiation, with ROI/cost-of-change/risk/time-to-value — delivered to (and ideally co-built with) the Economic Buyer. (evidence: [T03-S016, T03-S007])
- **入门 SOP（minimum viable steps）**:
  1. **Quantify the business value** — translate the discovered pain into a metric the buyer owns (cost today vs. future state; the "M" in MEDDIC). (evidence: [T03-S007])
     - **跳过后果**: no number = no business case; the EB has nothing to approve budget against. (evidence: [T03-S007])
  2. **Build the value narrative (Command of the Message)** — Before/After states, negative consequences of inaction, PBOs, required capabilities, and your differentiation vs. alternatives (including status quo). (evidence: [T03-S016])
     - **跳过后果**: you pitch features; the EB hears a vendor, not a business partner, and defaults to "no decision" or the cheapest option. (evidence: [T03-S016])
  3. **Get access to the Economic Buyer** — use the champion to identify and introduce the EB; if the champion can't/won't, that's a red flag. (evidence: [T03-S007])
     - **跳过后果**: you present the business case to the champion (no budget authority) → it never reaches the person who can fund it. (evidence: [T03-S007])
  4. **Speak the EB's language** — frame in cost, time-to-value, and *sure-ness* (team confidence the initiative will work); EBs care about top/bottom-line impact and risk, not your product. (evidence: [T03-S007])
     - **跳过后果**: a feature-heavy pitch to an outcome-focused EB falls flat; they don't see why *now* and why *you*. (evidence: [T03-S007])
  5. **Tie the case to a decision + de-risk** — connect the business case to the decision criteria/process and address risk/cost-of-change to clear the path to approval. (evidence: [T03-S016, T03-S033])
     - **跳过后果**: a compelling case with un-addressed risk stalls — FOMU (fear of messing up) freezes the EB into indecision. (evidence: [T03-S033])
- **资深路径（差异点）**:
  - **skip**: seniors **skip feature-selling to user buyers** as the path to a deal — they go *up* to business value and the EB; juniors over-invest in the friendly technical/user contact who can't fund the deal. (evidence: [T03-S016, T03-S007])
  - **optimize**: seniors **optimize the EB conversation** — they lead with "much-ness, soon-ness, sure-ness" (cost / time-to-value / confidence) and *co-build* the business case with the buyer so it's the buyer's case, not the vendor's. Juniors send an ROI deck and hope. (evidence: [T03-S007])
  - **add**: seniors **add early EB access** — they engage the EB early (not just at the end), and they explicitly **de-risk** to defeat FOMU (references, guarantees, phased rollout). Juniors meet the EB late (or never) and leave risk unaddressed. (evidence: [T03-S007, T03-S033])
- **近期变化（近 12 个月）**: 2024-2026 **budget-scrutiny + quota-attainment crisis** (Ebsta×Pavilion: 78% missed quota in 2025, up from 69%) pushed business-case rigor and "sell the ROI of *change* vs. status quo" harder; AI-savings claims are now a common (and scrutinized) PBO. The **value-narrative framework itself is stable.** 触发事件: macro budget tightening (2024-2026) + AI-ROI claims. (evidence: [T03-S016, T03-S033])
- **典型耗时**: 入门 SOP — days to assemble; one EB meeting. 资深路径 — value discovery is *continuous* (seniors gather EB-relevant metrics across many calls), but they reach the EB far earlier, compressing the overall path to budget.
- **关键工具（Track 02）**: Command of the Message value framework (artifact, 场景特化), MEDDPICC Metrics/EB fields (artifact), business-case/proposal tools (PandaDoc, 场景特化). (evidence: [T03-S016, T03-S007])
- **关键人物（Track 01）**: Force Management / John McMahon & John Kaplan (Command of the Message value framework, selling to the economic buyer); Anthony Iannarino (level-4 value creation). (evidence: [T03-S016])
- **常见失败模式**:
  - **Building the business case for the champion instead of the EB**: the champion can't approve budget; the case dies at their ceiling. Avoid: use the champion to *introduce* the EB; present value to the funder. (evidence: [T03-S007])
  - **Feature-dumping to an outcome-focused EB**: EBs care about cost/time-to-value/risk, not your feature list. Avoid: Before/After + PBOs + differentiation in *their* language. (evidence: [T03-S016, T03-S007])
  - **Leaving risk unaddressed → FOMU freezes the EB**: even a strong ROI case stalls if the buyer fears messing up. Avoid: de-risk explicitly (references, phased rollout, guarantees). (evidence: [T03-S033])
  - **"value / synergy / trusted advisor" buzzwords without quantification**: hollowed-out language signals a feature-seller in disguise. Avoid: every value claim carries a number the buyer agreed to. (evidence: [T03-S016])

### 6. Drive a deal to close with a Mutual Action Plan（用双向行动计划驱动成交）

- **One-liner**: After a verbal-yes, *co-build* a dated close plan that walks both sides through legal, security, procurement, and go-live — because **deals die in the paper process**, not at "yes." The late-stage de-stall motion. (evidence: [T03-S017, T03-S005])
- **Trigger**: A verbal commit / late-stage deal with a multi-step path to signature ahead (procurement, legal, security review). (evidence: [T03-S017])
- **Output**: A shared, dated Mutual Action Plan, co-owned by buyer + seller, executed step-by-step to an signed contract (with no late surprises). (evidence: [T03-S017])
- **入门 SOP（minimum viable steps）**:
  1. **Co-build the MAP with the buyer** — list every step from now to go-live (legal/MSA, security review, procurement, exec sign-off, implementation) with owners and dates, *together*. (evidence: [T03-S017])
     - **跳过后果**: a seller-built checklist reads as the vendor's agenda; the buyer doesn't engage and it becomes "just another document." (evidence: [T03-S017])
  2. **Map the Paper Process** — identify the procurement/legal/security path (MSA, redlines, SOC 2, DPA) explicitly. (evidence: [T03-S005])
     - **跳过后果**: "verbal yes" then the deal stalls for weeks in unmapped procurement/security you didn't see coming. (evidence: [T03-S005])
  3. **Pre-empt security/procurement gates** — surface SOC 2/DPA/vendor questionnaires *early* (these are table stakes / auto-disqualifiers in enterprise procurement). (evidence: [T03-S005])
     - **跳过后果**: a late-discovered security review or missing SOC 2 blows the close date / kills the deal at the finish line. (evidence: [T03-S005])
  4. **Keep the MAP live + track engagement** — review it on every call; in a digital sales room, watch which stakeholder went dark. (evidence: [T03-S017])
     - **跳过后果**: a stale MAP nobody updates hides the fact that the deal already stalled; you find out at quarter-end. (evidence: [T03-S017])
  5. **Defeat indecision (JOLT)** — when a qualified deal stalls late, treat it as *indecision* (FOMU), not lost-to-competitor: judge the indecision, offer a recommendation, limit options, take risk off the table. (evidence: [T03-S032, T03-S033])
     - **跳过后果**: you wait passively for the buyer to "get back to you"; the deal dies in no-decision limbo (40-60% of forecast losses, caveat CEB/Tethr). (evidence: [T03-S032])
- **资深路径（差异点）**:
  - **skip**: seniors **never build the MAP solo and email it** — they refuse to advance without the buyer co-authoring it; juniors hand over a polished seller-checklist and wonder why the buyer ghosts. (evidence: [T03-S017])
  - **optimize**: seniors **pull security/procurement forward** to the discovery/business-case stage instead of discovering it at negotiation; they pre-stage SOC 2/DPA so the paper process is a formality, not a cliff. Juniors hit procurement cold at the end. (evidence: [T03-S005])
  - **add**: seniors **add active indecision-management** — they diagnose late-stage stalls as FOMU and de-risk proactively (recommend a path, limit options, take risk off the table) rather than "circling back." Juniors mistake a stall for a lost deal or a slow buyer. (evidence: [T03-S032, T03-S033])
- **近期变化（近 12 个月）**: 2024-2026, **AI-generated MAPs** are now standard in digital-sales-room tools (Dock/Accord/Momentum, the latter Salesforce-acquired Mar 2026) and **SOC 2/DPA/security review became a default procurement gate** (06-glossary hand-off) — pushing security pre-emption earlier in the deal. The **MAP-as-co-built-close-plan + JOLT de-risking principles are stable.** 触发事件: digital-sales-room AI + security-review normalization. (evidence: [T03-S017, T03-S005])
- **典型耗时**: 入门 SOP — paper process commonly runs 2-8 weeks (enterprise legal/security). 资深路径 — seniors compress it by pre-staging gates, often halving the post-verbal-yes timeline; a missed security review can add 4-8 weeks or kill the deal.
- **关键工具（Track 02）**: Mutual Action Plan / digital sales room (artifact + tool: Dock/GetAccept/Accord, 必备), Docusign (e-sign, 必备), CPQ/proposal (PandaDoc, 场景特化). (evidence: [T03-S017])
- **关键人物（Track 01）**: Matthew Dixon & Ted McKenna (JOLT — defeating indecision at the late stage); MEDDPICC's Paper Process (McMahon/Whyte). (evidence: [T03-S032, T03-S005])
- **常见失败模式**:
  - **Building the MAP alone and handing it over**: buyers read it as the seller's agenda and disengage. Avoid: co-author it live with the buyer; keep it in a shared room. (evidence: [T03-S017])
  - **"We got a verbal yes, the deal's done"**: deals die in the paper process (redlines/security/procurement). Avoid: map and pre-empt the procurement/security/legal path early. (evidence: [T03-S005])
  - **Discovering a security review / missing SOC 2 at the finish line**: a late security gate slips or kills the close. Avoid: surface SOC 2/DPA/questionnaires at the business-case stage. (evidence: [T03-S005])
  - **Treating a late-stage stall as a lost deal or a slow buyer**: it's usually indecision (FOMU). Avoid: JOLT it — recommend, limit options, de-risk; don't passively "circle back." (evidence: [T03-S033, T03-S032])

### 7. Forecast a quarter (rep side)（预测一个季度——销售个人侧）

- **One-liner**: Turn a pile of open deals into a *defensible* number — inspect each deal, categorize it honestly (commit / best case / pipeline), and own the commit. The discipline that separates a trusted rep from "happy ears." (evidence: [T03-S018, T03-S022])
- **Trigger**: Quarter/period start and the recurring (usually weekly) forecast cadence. (evidence: [T03-S019])
- **Output**: A per-deal forecast category with a defensible close date, rolling up to a committed number the rep will be held to. (evidence: [T03-S019, T03-S022])
- **入门 SOP（minimum viable steps）**:
  1. **Inspect each deal against evidence** — for every open opp, ask the deal-inspection questions: what % of the buyer's broader problem does this solve? has someone with decision power acknowledged the problem? what precedent exists for approving this spend? what changed since last contact? (evidence: [T03-S018])
     - **跳过后果**: you forecast on optimism, not evidence; the number is fiction and you miss. (evidence: [T03-S018])
  2. **Confirm the close date is real** — tie it to the buyer's decision process / a critical event, not a hopeful guess. (evidence: [T03-S019])
     - **跳过后果**: deals "slip" out of the period repeatedly, eroding forecast trust. (evidence: [T03-S019])
  3. **Categorize honestly** — Commit (90-95%, a near-promise), Best Case (50-70%, material risk), Pipeline (15-30%, longshots); categories are *confidence buckets*, orthogonal to deal stage. (evidence: [T03-S022])
     - **跳过后果**: miscategorized deals (best-case dressed as commit) blow the team forecast and your credibility. (evidence: [T03-S022])
  4. **Respect the commit bank account** — commit only up to (never above) the total value of your genuinely-committed deals. (evidence: [T03-S018])
     - **跳过后果**: over-committing to look good → a public miss; under-committing (sandbagging) erodes trust the other way. (evidence: [T03-S018])
- **资深路径（差异点）**:
  - **skip**: seniors **skip the happy-ears optimism** — they don't commit a deal just because a call "felt great"; they need MEDDPICC evidence (named EB, mapped process). Juniors commit on vibes. (evidence: [T03-S018, T03-S005])
  - **optimize**: seniors **optimize categorization with evidence** — a deal is Commit only if they can name the EB, the close date ties to the buyer's decision process, and the paper process is mapped. They self-inspect *before* the manager does. (evidence: [T03-S018, T03-S022])
  - **add**: seniors **add commit discipline** (commit-bank account: never forecast above committed value) and proactively flag risk early. Juniors hide slipping deals until quarter-end. (evidence: [T03-S018])
- **近期变化（近 12 个月）**: 2024-2026, **AI forecasting** (Clari/Gong AI layer) now cross-checks rep-entered categories against activity/conversation signals — flagging deals with no multithreading / no EB engagement / gone-dark — so "the tool visualizes the lie faster" if reps sandbag. **ASC 606** keeps bookings≠revenue, which shapes how quota/commit map to recognized revenue. The **commit/best-case discipline itself is stable.** 触发事件: AI forecast tooling + rev-rec rigor. (evidence: [T03-S018, T03-S030])
- **典型耗时**: 入门 SOP — ~1-2 hours weekly to inspect + categorize a book. 资深路径 — similar time, but a senior's forecast is "boring" (accurate) because the evidence work happened continuously across the quarter, not crammed at the deadline.
- **关键工具（Track 02）**: Clari (forecasting & deal inspection, 必备), CRM (必备), Gong (signal cross-check, 必备), MEDDPICC scorecard (artifact). (evidence: [T03-S018, T03-S030])
- **关键人物（Track 01）**: John McMahon ("forecast from evidence, not optimism"; deal inspection) is the canonical voice; Clari operationalizes it. (evidence: [T03-S018])
- **常见失败模式**:
  - **Happy ears — committing deals on optimism, not evidence**: "the buyer loved it" is not a forecast. Avoid: commit only with MEDDPICC evidence (named EB, mapped decision process, real close date). (evidence: [T03-S018, T03-S005])
  - **Miscategorizing best-case as commit**: blows the team number and your credibility. Avoid: treat categories as confidence buckets with explicit thresholds (commit = 90-95%). (evidence: [T03-S022])
  - **Hopeful close dates → repeated slips**: erodes forecast trust deal by deal. Avoid: tie the close date to the buyer's decision process / a critical event. (evidence: [T03-S019])
  - **Sandbagging (chronically closing above commit)**: hiding commits in best-case erodes trust the other direction and distorts capacity planning. Avoid: forecast to the commit-bank — honest, not gamed. (evidence: [T03-S018])
- **来源**:
  - [Primary] Clari — 6 questions to ask in every deal inspection: https://www.clari.com/blog/6-questions-to-ask-in-every-deal-inspection/ (T03-S018, collected 2026-06-11)
  - [Primary] Clari — what a "commit" really means: https://www.clari.com/blog/do-your-sales-reps-know-what-a-commit-really-means-1/ (T03-S022)
  - [Primary] Clari — the ultimate guide to your forecast call: https://www.clari.com/blog/the-ultimate-guide-to-your-forecast-call/ (T03-S019)
- **Last_updated**: 2026-06-11. **Decay risk**: **medium** (AI forecast cross-checking is changing the *enforcement*; the commit/best-case discipline is stable).

### 8. Negotiate with procurement（与采购对线 / 谈判）

- **One-liner**: When the champion's done and the deal hits procurement/legal, protect margin by **trading concessions** (not discounting) — treating negotiation as a process you ran all along, not a price event at the end. (evidence: [T03-S023, T03-S025])
- **Trigger**: Verbal-yes secured; the deal is handed to procurement/legal for terms and price. (evidence: [T03-S023])
- **Output**: An executed contract at protected margin, where any price concession was *traded* for buyer-side value (term length, prepay, case study, expansion commitment), not given away. (evidence: [T03-S023, T03-S025])
- **入门 SOP（minimum viable steps）**:
  1. **Establish value before price** — by the time you reach procurement, the business value/ROI should already be agreed (negotiation is the *extension* of a process, not its start). (evidence: [T03-S023])
     - **跳过后果**: with no agreed value, procurement anchors purely on price and you have nothing to defend margin with. (evidence: [T03-S023])
  2. **Decode procurement's real drivers** — uncover the business + personal interests behind their requirements (hit a budget number? show a saving? de-risk the vendor?). (evidence: [T03-S023])
     - **跳过后果**: you negotiate against a stated position instead of the underlying interest → you concede on the wrong thing. (evidence: [T03-S023])
  3. **Build a give/get playlist** — predefine what you can trade and what you want in return (anchors + multiple options): discount ⇄ longer term / prepay / reference / case study / expansion / faster signature. (evidence: [T03-S023, T03-S025])
     - **跳过后果**: with no give/get list ready, you cave on price under pressure and get nothing back. (evidence: [T03-S023])
  4. **Trade, never just discount** — every concession is contingent ("if you can do X, I can do Y"); use calibrated questions / tactical empathy to keep it collaborative. (evidence: [T03-S023, T03-S024])
     - **跳过后果**: unilateral discounts train the buyer to keep pushing and signal your price was inflated. (evidence: [T03-S025])
- **资深路径（差异点）**:
  - **skip**: seniors **skip caving on the first price ask** — they don't drop price to "save the deal"; they slow down and trade. Juniors discount reflexively when procurement pushes. (evidence: [T03-S025, T03-S023])
  - **optimize**: seniors **optimize by negotiating value throughout the deal** (not as a final event) — so procurement is a formality, and they run a prepared give/get playlist with anchors. Juniors arrive at procurement cold with no tradeable inventory. (evidence: [T03-S023])
  - **add**: seniors **re-engage the EB/champion when procurement stalls** (procurement often can't say yes, only no) and use tactical empathy / calibrated questions to expand the pie. Juniors negotiate against the procurement gatekeeper as if they're the decision-maker. (evidence: [T03-S024, T03-S023])
- **近期变化（近 12 个月）**: 2024-2026 **pricing-negotiation discipline is a top-3 topic-heat theme** (30MPC "The Worst Pricing Advice In B2B Sales" + a new Negotiation course; OutBound 2026 theme = buyer resistance/objections), driven by the budget-scrutiny macro (78% missed quota 2025). The **trade-don't-discount / value-throughout principles are decades-stable** (Voss, INKED). 触发事件: macro budget tightening → harder procurement (2024-2026). (evidence: [T03-S025, T03-S023])
- **典型耗时**: 入门 SOP — procurement/legal negotiation runs days to a few weeks. 资深路径 — seniors shorten it (value pre-agreed, give/get ready) and protect ~more margin; juniors get dragged into multi-round price erosion.
- **关键工具（Track 02）**: give/get playlist (artifact), CPQ (PandaDoc/Revenue Cloud, 场景特化), Black Swan tactical-empathy toolkit (artifact), Docusign (e-sign, 必备). (evidence: [T03-S023, T03-S024])
- **关键人物（Track 01）**: Chris Voss (tactical empathy, calibrated questions, "never split the difference"), Jeb Blount (INKED — 7 rules of sales negotiation, give/get), Force Management (value-based procurement negotiation). (evidence: [T03-S024, T03-S025, T03-S023])
- **常见失败模式**:
  - **Caving on price to "save the deal"**: unilateral discounts train the buyer to keep pushing and admit your price was soft. Avoid: every concession is contingent — trade, don't give. (evidence: [T03-S025])
  - **Treating negotiation as a final event**: if you didn't build value all along, procurement anchors on price. Avoid: negotiate/validate value from the start of the deal. (evidence: [T03-S023])
  - **Negotiating against procurement as the decision-maker**: procurement can usually only say no; the EB owns yes. Avoid: re-engage the EB/champion when procurement stalls on terms. (evidence: [T03-S023])
  - **No give/get playlist ready → concede the wrong things under pressure**: improvising mid-negotiation loses margin. Avoid: predefine tradeable gives + the gets you want (term/prepay/reference/expansion). (evidence: [T03-S023])
- **来源**:
  - [Primary] Force Management — negotiation tactics with procurement (process, not event): https://www.forcemanagement.com/seller-blog/two-sales-negotiation-tactics-to-use-with-procurement (T03-S023, collected 2026-06-11)
  - [Primary] Black Swan (Voss) — how to negotiate with procurement: https://www.blackswanltd.com/newsletter/how-to-negotiate-with-procurement (T03-S024)
  - [Primary] Sales Gravy (Blount) — INKED 7 rules of sales negotiation: https://salesgravy.com/getting-ink-7-rules-of-sales-negotiation/ (T03-S025)
- **Last_updated**: 2026-06-11. **Decay risk**: **medium** (macro budget pressure intensifies the motion; the trade-don't-discount craft is stable).

### 9. Account planning / land-and-expand（客户计划 / 落地后扩张）

- **One-liner**: After the first win, deliberately *land small then expand* — build an account plan (stakeholders, whitespace, expansion path), prove value in QBRs, and grow seats/products (the NRR engine). The post-sale revenue motion that's cheaper than new logos. (evidence: [T03-S026, T03-S027])
- **Trigger**: A closed-won named/strategic account with renewal + expansion horizon; the start of the post-sale relationship. (evidence: [T03-S027])
- **Output**: A living account plan validated each QBR — mapped stakeholders, a whitespace map, and a sequenced expansion path that drives net revenue retention (NRR >100%). (evidence: [T03-S026, T03-S027])
- **入门 SOP（minimum viable steps）**:
  1. **Land a deliberate beachhead** — win a focused first deal you can deliver and prove value on (not the biggest-possible first contract). (evidence: [T03-S026])
     - **跳过后果**: an oversized, under-delivered first deal churns; you lose the logo before you can expand. (evidence: [T03-S026])
  2. **Build the account plan** — map stakeholders (incl. ones outside the first deal), document the relationship, and define the expansion strategy. (evidence: [T03-S027])
     - **跳过后果**: no plan = reactive renewals; you miss expansion and get blindsided by a champion who left. (evidence: [T03-S027])
  3. **Run whitespace analysis** — identify un-penetrated products/teams/use-cases inside the account proactively (not just at renewal). (evidence: [T03-S026])
     - **跳过后果**: you leave expansion revenue on the table and let a competitor land in the whitespace. (evidence: [T03-S026])
  4. **Validate value in QBRs** — use the Quarterly Business Review to prove delivered outcomes, then open expansion conversations from a position of proven value. (evidence: [T03-S027, T03-S026])
     - **跳过后果**: pitching expansion without proven value = a cold upsell the buyer resists; renewal itself becomes at-risk. (evidence: [T03-S027])
  5. **Sequence the expansion** — convert whitespace + proven value into a land-and-expand motion (more seats, new products/teams) over the account lifecycle. (evidence: [T03-S026])
     - **跳过后果**: a flat account with no expansion drags NRR below 100% — the SaaS growth killer. (evidence: [T03-S026])
- **资深路径（差异点）**:
  - **skip**: seniors **skip the cold expansion pitch** — they never propose more before proving value on what's already sold; juniors push upsell at the first renewal regardless of delivered outcomes. (evidence: [T03-S027])
  - **optimize**: seniors **optimize by landing small on purpose** — they pick a beachhead they can over-deliver on, because a proven first win is the cheapest path to expansion. Juniors chase the biggest first deal and over-promise. (evidence: [T03-S026])
  - **add**: seniors **add a whitespace map + account-wide multithreading** — they engage stakeholders beyond the first buyer early so the account survives a champion's departure and expansion paths are pre-built. Juniors stay single-threaded on the original contact. (evidence: [T03-S026, T03-S028])
- **近期变化（近 12 个月）**: 2024-2026, **expansion is now the dominant revenue source** (Ebsta×Pavilion: expansion = 52% of new revenue) amid the new-logo squeeze, so account-planning/NRR rigor rose sharply; AI now assists whitespace/expansion-signal detection. The **bowtie / land-and-expand framework is established** (Winning by Design). 触发事件: macro new-logo squeeze → expansion focus (2024-2026). (evidence: [T03-S026, T03-S027])
- **典型耗时**: 入门 SOP — account plan built post-close, reviewed each QBR (quarterly). 资深路径 — continuous whitespace/stakeholder maintenance; seniors treat the account as an ongoing campaign, not a quarterly chore.
- **关键工具（Track 02）**: account plan (artifact), Miller Heiman Blue Sheet (artifact, strategic accounts, 场景特化), bowtie/SPICED canvas (artifact, 场景特化), CRM (必备). (evidence: [T03-S026, T03-S027])
- **关键人物（Track 01）**: Jacco van der Kooij (bowtie — acquisition+retention+expansion as one system; expansion playbook); Force Management (strategic account management plan); Robert Miller & Stephen Heiman (Blue Sheet, legacy). (evidence: [T03-S026, T03-S027])
- **常见失败模式**:
  - **Pitching expansion before proving value**: a cold upsell at renewal the buyer resists — and it can sour the renewal itself. Avoid: validate delivered outcomes in a QBR first, then expand from proven value. (evidence: [T03-S027])
  - **Over-sized first deal that under-delivers → logo churn**: you lose the account before you can expand. Avoid: land a deliberate, deliverable beachhead. (evidence: [T03-S026])
  - **Single-threaded account → champion leaves, account at risk**: relying on the original buyer. Avoid: multithread across the account early; build a whitespace + stakeholder map. (evidence: [T03-S028])
  - **No whitespace analysis → flat NRR + competitor lands in the gap**: expansion revenue left on the table. Avoid: proactively map un-penetrated products/teams/use-cases. (evidence: [T03-S026])
- **来源**:
  - [Primary] Winning by Design — expansion & growth playbook (account planning + whitespace + QBR): https://winningbydesign.com/services/expansion-growth-playbook/ (T03-S026, collected 2026-06-11)
  - [Primary] Force Management — build the ultimate strategic account management plan: https://www.forcemanagement.com/blog/build-the-ultimate-strategic-account-management-plan (T03-S027)
  - [Reference] Highspot — multithreading (account-wide): https://www.highspot.com/blog/multithreading-in-sales/ (T03-S028)
- **Last_updated**: 2026-06-11. **Decay risk**: **medium** (expansion-focus intensity + AI whitespace detection shift; the land-and-expand/bowtie framework is stable).

### 10. Run a pipeline / deal review (manager side)（运营 pipeline / deal review——管理者侧）

- **One-liner**: A manager's weekly inspection that finds *blind spots* in deals fast — not a box-checking recap. Done right it covers 10+ deals in 30 minutes and produces coaching, not theater. (evidence: [T03-S021, T03-S020])
- **Trigger**: The weekly/biweekly team forecast + pipeline cadence (often alternating forecast-focus and pipeline-creation-focus weeks). (evidence: [T03-S020])
- **Output**: Inspected pipeline (deal health + future-quarter coverage) → an updated team forecast + a specific coaching action per at-risk deal. (evidence: [T03-S020, T03-S021])
- **入门 SOP（minimum viable steps）**:
  1. **Define stages + exit criteria** — establish what constitutes progress at each stage so "stage" means something. (evidence: [T03-S021])
     - **跳过后果**: without exit criteria, reps self-assign stages by feel; the pipeline is noise and the forecast is unanchored. (evidence: [T03-S021])
  2. **Select big deals + strike-zone deals** — focus the review on high-value and decision-moment deals, not every opp. (evidence: [T03-S021])
     - **跳过后果**: reviewing all deals shallowly wastes 30 min on 3-5 deals and misses the ones that matter. (evidence: [T03-S021])
  3. **Reps recap each stage in one sentence** — how the exit criteria were met + the plan for the next two stages (minimum recap). (evidence: [T03-S021])
     - **跳过后果**: 10-minute deal monologues + a multitasking manager = a meeting nobody learns from. (evidence: [T03-S021])
  4. **Manager asks blind-spot questions** — for past stages probe the *method* of achievement; for future stages challenge the *viability* of the plan ("rollercoaster attendant checking safety," not interrogator). (evidence: [T03-S021])
     - **跳过后果**: no probing = optimism goes unchallenged; happy-ears deals stay in commit until they miss. (evidence: [T03-S021])
- **资深路径（差异点）**:
  - **skip**: senior managers **skip the long recaps** — they demand the *minimum* recap to find blind spots, hitting 10+ deals in 30 min; junior managers let reps narrate every detail and review 3-5 deals. (evidence: [T03-S021])
  - **optimize**: senior managers **optimize with blind-spot questions over box-checking** — they probe how exit criteria were *actually* met (can you name the EB? has decision-power acknowledged the problem? what precedent for this spend?) instead of checking that CRM fields are filled. (evidence: [T03-S021, T03-S018])
  - **add**: senior managers **add exit-criteria gating + a what-changed pulse** — deals can't advance stage without meeting criteria, and every deal gets "what changed since last contact?" Junior managers inspect the field-completion, not the deal. (evidence: [T03-S021, T03-S018])
- **近期变化（近 12 个月）**: 2024-2026, **AI deal-inspection** (Clari/Gong) auto-flags at-risk deals (no multithreading, gone-dark, no EB engagement) *before* the review, so the human review focuses on coaching the flagged deals — but this risks **surveillance theater** if managers nitpick scores instead of coaching behavior. The **inspect-for-blind-spots discipline is stable.** 触发事件: AI deal-coaching layer maturing. (evidence: [T03-S020, T03-S030])
- **典型耗时**: 入门 SOP — 30-60 min weekly team review + ~weekly 1:1 deal inspections. 资深路径 — same cadence, far higher deal-throughput per minute (10+ deals/30 min) because exit criteria + one-sentence recaps do the structuring.
- **关键工具（Track 02）**: Clari (deal inspection + pipeline review, 必备), Gong (AI deal flags, 必备), MEDDPICC scorecard (artifact, the shared review language), CRM (必备). (evidence: [T03-S020, T03-S018])
- **关键人物（Track 01）**: John McMahon ("what do you need managers for if they're not coaching deals along?"; deal inspection) is the canonical voice; 30MPC details the rep-facing review mechanics; Clari operationalizes it. (evidence: [T03-S021, T03-S018])
- **常见失败模式**:
  - **Box-checking deal review — checking that CRM fields are filled, not whether the deal is real**: produces compliance, not coaching. Avoid: ask blind-spot questions about *quality* (name the EB, prove the pain), enforce exit criteria. (evidence: [T03-S021, T03-S018])
  - **10-minute recaps with a multitasking manager**: reviews 3-5 deals, teaches nothing. Avoid: one-sentence-per-stage recaps; aim for 10+ deals in 30 min. (evidence: [T03-S021])
  - **No exit criteria → stages are meaningless**: reps self-assign stage by feel and the forecast is unanchored. Avoid: define exit criteria per stage and gate advancement on them. (evidence: [T03-S021])
  - **AI flags used to nitpick scores instead of coach behavior → surveillance theater**: reps game the metrics, adoption becomes compliance. Avoid: use AI flags to *start* a coaching conversation, not to police talk-ratios. (evidence: [T03-S030])
- **来源**:
  - [Primary] 30MPC — 4 steps to run a pipeline review that doesn't suck: https://www.30mpc.com/newsletter/4-steps-to-run-a-pipeline-review-that-doesnt-suck (T03-S021, collected 2026-06-11)
  - [Primary] Clari — how to run a sales pipeline review: https://www.clari.com/blog/sales-pipeline-review/ (T03-S020)
  - [Primary] Clari — 6 deal-inspection questions: https://www.clari.com/blog/6-questions-to-ask-in-every-deal-inspection/ (T03-S018)
- **Last_updated**: 2026-06-11. **Decay risk**: **low** (the inspect-for-blind-spots cadence is durable; the AI-flag layer on top is the moving part).

### 11. Multithread the buying committee（多线程触达买方委员会）

- **One-liner**: Engage the 6-10 stakeholders who actually decide — in parallel — instead of betting the deal on a single champion. A cross-cutting motion woven through discovery → qualify → close; the single biggest structural difference between junior and senior deals. (evidence: [T03-S028, T03-S029])
- **Trigger**: Any complex deal with more than one stakeholder — i.e. effectively all enterprise B2B deals (HBR: ~6.8 buyers, directional). (evidence: [T03-S029])
- **Output**: An engaged, mapped set of stakeholders (economic / user / technical buyers + champion + blockers) progressing in parallel, with no single point of failure. (evidence: [T03-S028, T03-S030])
- **入门 SOP（minimum viable steps）**:
  1. **Map the buying committee** — identify the roles: Economic Buyer, User Buyer(s), Technical Buyer, Champion, Coach, Blocker(s). (evidence: [T03-S028])
     - **跳过后果**: you don't know who's missing; a hidden blocker or un-engaged EB surfaces late and kills the deal. (evidence: [T03-S028])
  2. **Engage beyond the first contact** — proactively reach the other stakeholders (often via the champion / a multithread-ask in discovery). (evidence: [T03-S028])
     - **跳过后果**: single-threaded — if your one contact leaves, goes cold, or lacks power, the deal collapses. (evidence: [T03-S028])
  3. **Tailor the message per stakeholder** — EB hears business value, technical buyer hears validation, user buyer hears day-to-day impact. (evidence: [T03-S016])
     - **跳过后果**: one generic pitch resonates with nobody; stakeholders don't self-identify a reason to champion you. (evidence: [T03-S016])
  4. **Build internal consensus via a Mobilizer** — find the stakeholder willing to challenge the status quo and drive alignment, not just the friendly "Talker." (evidence: [T03-S029])
     - **跳过后果**: a friendly contact with no will to drive change = no internal momentum; the committee defaults to no-decision. (evidence: [T03-S029, T03-S032])
- **资深路径（差异点）**:
  - **skip**: seniors **skip the single-champion comfort zone** — they refuse to let a deal depend on one relationship; juniors get cozy with one friendly contact and call it a champion. (evidence: [T03-S028])
  - **optimize**: seniors **optimize "map-then-sequence"** — they map the full committee early and *sequence* outreach (champion → coach → EB), proactively engaging multiple threads; won deals carry ~2× the buyer contacts of lost ones. Juniors react to whoever replies. (evidence: [T03-S030, T03-S028])
  - **add**: seniors **add a coach→EB path + blocker engagement** — they use the coach to reach the EB early and engage blockers before blockers engage them. Juniors avoid blockers and never reach power. (evidence: [T03-S028, T03-S029])
- **近期变化（近 12 个月）**: 2024-2026 multithreading is a **top topic-heat theme** (30MPC "How to Multithread and Get to Power"), reinforced by first-party data (Gong: won deals have ~2× the buyer contacts; multithreading lifts >$50K-deal win-rate materially, caveat 第一方); AI tools now surface "single-threaded / no-EB-engagement" risk automatically. The buying-committee reality (no single buyer) is **structurally stable and only intensifying** (buying groups growing). 触发事件: AI deal-risk flagging + buying-committee growth. (evidence: [T03-S030, T03-S029])
- **典型耗时**: 入门 SOP — woven across the whole deal cycle (6-18 months enterprise). 资深路径 — seniors front-load committee mapping in the first 1-2 calls, so multithreading compounds early instead of scrambling for the EB at the end.
- **关键工具（Track 02）**: MEDDPICC stakeholder fields / Blue Sheet (artifact, the committee map), Gong (multithreading/EB-engagement signals, 必备), digital sales room (engagement tracking, 必备). (evidence: [T03-S028, T03-S030])
- **关键人物（Track 01）**: Matthew Dixon & Brent Adamson (The Challenger Customer — Mobilizers vs Talkers vs Blockers); HBR/Toman (6.8-buyers thesis); Gong Labs (multithreading data). (evidence: [T03-S029, T03-S030])
- **常见失败模式**:
  - **Single-threaded deal — relying on one champion/contact**: if they leave or go cold, the deal dies. Avoid: map the full committee and engage ≥3 stakeholders in parallel. (evidence: [T03-S028])
  - **Selling to "the decision maker" (singular)**: enterprise B2B has a 6-10-person committee, no single buyer. Avoid: map and multithread the whole group. (evidence: [T03-S029])
  - **Chasing the friendly Talker instead of the Mobilizer**: the agreeable contact who won't drive change produces no internal momentum. Avoid: find the stakeholder willing to challenge the status quo and build consensus. (evidence: [T03-S029])
  - **Avoiding blockers until they surface late**: an un-engaged blocker can veto at the finish line. Avoid: engage blockers early — they can flip to champions if won. (evidence: [T03-S028])

## Phase 2 提炼提示

### 反复出现 ≥ 3 个 workflow 都包含的步骤（候选 playbook 通则）

- **「Find + quantify the pain before anything else」** — 出现于 workflows **1 (discovery), 2 (MEDDPICC: Identify Pain+Metrics), 4 (POC: business pain before demo), 5 (business case)**. → 候选 playbook 「如果 discovery 浅 / 没有量化痛点 → 先把 cost-of-problem 挖到买家亲口说出数字,再谈方案」。(evidence: [T03-S001, T03-S003, T03-S005, T03-S016])
- **「Name + reach the Economic Buyer」** — 出现于 workflows **2 (qualify), 5 (business case), 8 (negotiate: re-engage EB), 11 (multithread: coach→EB)**. → 候选 playbook 「如果你说不出 EB 的名字 → 这单还没 qualify;用 champion 早做 EB 引荐,别在 procurement 阶段才发现没碰过决策人」。(evidence: [T03-S007, T03-S005])
- **「Multithread / map the committee, never single-thread」** — 出现于 workflows **1 (multithread-ask in discovery), 6 (MAP across stakeholders), 9 (account-wide), 11 (core)**. → 候选 playbook 「如果一单只靠一个联系人 → 立刻 map 6-10 人 buying committee 并并行 engage ≥3 人」。(evidence: [T03-S028, T03-S030])
- **「Set exit/success criteria up front + lock a dated next step」** — 出现于 workflows **1 (5-min drill next step), 4 (POC success criteria), 6 (MAP dated steps), 10 (stage exit criteria)**. → 候选 playbook 「任何推进动作前先约定:什么算成功 / 下一步是什么 / 谁在什么时候做」。(evidence: [T03-S001, T03-S015, T03-S017, T03-S021])
- **「Qualify hard / disqualify early — evidence over optimism」** — 出现于 workflows **2 (MEDDPICC), 4 (qualify the POC), 7 (forecast: evidence not happy-ears), 10 (review: blind-spot Qs)**. → 候选 playbook 「如果填不出 EB/pain/decision-process → 别投入资源,disqualify 比 bad deal 好」。(evidence: [T03-S005, T03-S018])
- **「The real competitor is no-decision / status quo — de-risk to defeat FOMU」** — 出现于 workflows **1, 5, 6, 11** (每个都把 no-decision 列为头号失败模式). → 候选 playbook 「丢单先问『是不是输给 no-decision』而非『输给谁』;late-stage stall = 用 JOLT 去风险,不是被动等回复」。(evidence: [T03-S032, T03-S033])

### 入门 SOP 和资深路径之间最大的差距（候选 心智模型）

- **入门 SOP 平均 4-5 步;资深路径几乎不压缩步数,而是把每步「做得更深 + 更早 + 更挑剔」** —— 与多数工程行业「资深人压缩步数」不同,B2B 销售的资深差异是**质量与时序差异**,不是步数差异。资深人不跳 discovery/qualify/multithread,而是:(a) **更早**触达 EB / pre-empt security / map committee(时序前置);(b) **更挑剔**地 qualify-out(disqualify 快、不追没 EB 的单、不给没 success criteria 的 POC);(c) **更深**地量化痛点 / test champion / trade(不 accept vague pain、不假设 champion、不裸 discount)。→ **心智模型候选:「资深销售的杠杆不是少做几步,而是把对的事做在对的时间、并狠心砍掉没赢面的单」(quality + timing + ruthless qualification over shortcuts)**。
- **最频繁的「资深 skip」是同一类:skip 给没 qualify 的人投入资源** —— skip pitch to unqualified (W1)、skip pursuing no-EB deals (W2)、skip spray-everyone (W3)、skip POC for unqualified (W4)、skip feature-selling to users (W5)、skip building MAP solo (W6)、skip happy-ears commits (W7)、skip caving on price (W8)、skip cold expansion (W9)、skip long recaps (W10)、skip single-champion (W11)。→ **心智模型候选:「最大的资深技能是『不做』—— 不在没赢面/没决策人/没 success-criteria 的地方浪费动作」(disciplined subtraction)**。
- **最频繁的「资深 add」也是同一类:add 时序前置 + 多线程** —— add pre-call research + multithread-ask (W1)、add early EB access (W5)、add pre-empt security early (W6)、add whitespace + account-multithread (W9)、add coach→EB path (W11)。→ **心智模型候选:「把所有关键动作(EB 触达 / security / committee map / value 量化)从 deal 后期前移到前期」(front-load the late-stage risks)**。

### 近期工作流变化的根本原因（变化触发分布）

- **触发事件类型分布（11 workflows）**:
  - **AI(新工具)驱动 = 主导** — 8/11 workflows 的近期变化由 AI 触发:conversation-intelligence coaching (W1)、auto-MEDDIC capture (W2)、AI SDR collapse→hybrid (W3)、AI-product POC + SE coaching (W4)、AI-ROI claims (W5)、AI-generated MAP (W6)、AI forecast cross-check (W7)、AI deal-flagging (W10/W11)。**AI 是本行业当前头号外部驱动力**,但呈双面:既是 augmentation(call prep / note-taking / CRM 回填 / coaching)又是 backlash 源(AI SDR 群发 → 邮件送达率崩溃 → 11x/Artisan 翻车)。
  - **法规(法规变化)驱动 = 1 个高 decay 热点** — W3 (outbound) 受 TCPA circuit split (FCC 24-24 eff. 2025-01 → 第五巡回 2026 否决) + CCPA/CASL/CAN-SPAM 驱动;06-glossary 已标这是 12 月内仍在演变的硬时效层。
  - **宏观(行业事件)驱动 = 3 个** — budget-scrutiny / quota-attainment crisis (78% missed quota 2025, up from 69%) 推高 business-case 严谨度 (W5)、pricing-negotiation 纪律 (W8)、expansion/NRR 焦点 (W9, expansion=52% of new revenue)。
- **主要驱动力 → Phase 2 应识别的「外部驱动力」**:**①AI(augmentation vs backlash 双面,高 decay,影响 W1-W11 的工具层)** + **②outbound 合规法规(高 decay,geographic-specific,影响 W3)** + **③宏观效率/budget 压力(中 decay,影响 W5/W8/W9 的强度)**。稳态内核(discovery/qualify/negotiate/multithread/close 的核心动作链)几十年不变 → Phase 2.8 应明说「**工作流的 motion 稳定,变的是 AI 工具层 + 合规法规层**;update 时优先刷 W3(法规)+ 所有 workflow 的 AI 工具/采用率字段」。
