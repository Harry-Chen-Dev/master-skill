## 详细 Canon 条目 — Irreducible Core 7（按流派）

### 提问诊断派 (ask: diagnose before prescribe)

#### 📖 1. SPIN Selling — Neil Rackham (1988)

- **Author**: Neil Rackham (founder, Huthwaite). With Track 01 figures.
- **Year**: 1988 (HarperCollins; still in print, never superseded).
- **Type**: research-based monograph.
- **One-liner**: The empirical bedrock of consultative selling — proves that in *complex/large* sales, deep questioning beats closing tactics.
- **核心论点 (3-5)**:
  1. In large sales, the Situation→Problem→Implication→Need-payoff question sequence correlates with success. (evidence: [T04-S001])
  2. Classic "closing techniques" are counterproductive in large/complex deals (they raise resistance). (evidence: [T04-S001, T04-S002])
  3. Top performers spend disproportionate effort on *Implication* and *Need-payoff* questions, getting buyers to articulate value themselves. (evidence: [T04-S001])
- **读完得到什么**: A repeatable discovery question architecture + why product-feature-dumping fails.
- **难度**: 入门-进阶. **如果只读 1 章**: the Implication/Need-payoff chapters.
- **Endorsement evidence**: `[type: course_syllabus]` Huthwaite International runs it as a live methodology to this day (evidence: [T04-S001]); `[type: blog_secondary]` named "world's most renowned sales methodology," cross-referenced in nearly every methodology comparison (evidence: [T04-S003]); `[type: figure_short]` routinely cited as the discovery standard on 30MPC and by enablement leaders (evidence: [T04-S034]). **Endorsement count: ≥5 (strong canon).**
- **争议**: research is from the 1970s-80s (telephone + field, pre-SaaS). The *principles* are timeless but examples are dated; some argue Challenger/Gap "upgraded" SPIN for the insight era. (evidence: [T04-S004, T04-S010])
- **继承关系**: foundational ancestor of Solution Selling, Gap Selling, and the discovery half of every modern method. (evidence: [T04-S010, T04-S020])
- **可信度**: high. **Decay risk**: low.

#### 📖 6. Gap Selling — Keenan (2018)

- **Author**: Keenan (Jim Keenan), A Sales Growth Company. With Track 01 figures.
- **Year**: 2018 (self-published; Amazon #1 in sales).
- **One-liner**: A modern, blunt re-statement of problem-centric selling — buyers only change when there's a business problem worth solving.
- **核心论点 (3-5)**:
  1. Sell the **gap** between *current state* (what's broken, what it costs, root cause) and *future state*, never the product. (evidence: [T04-S010, T04-S011])
  2. Most reps under-diagnose; deep problem discovery is the entire job. (evidence: [T04-S010])
  3. Price/objections/closing problems are usually *symptoms of a weak gap*, not standalone issues. (evidence: [T04-S011])
- **读完得到什么**: A problem-first discovery discipline + the confidence to disqualify "no problem" deals.
- **难度**: 入门-进阶. **Endorsement evidence**: `[type: figure_long]` Keenan runs a training company + long-form content around it (evidence: [T04-S010]); `[type: blog_secondary]` Amazon #1 bestseller, widely cross-cited as the modern discovery text (evidence: [T04-S011]); `[type: figure_short]` frequently named alongside SPIN/Challenger in methodology comparisons (evidence: [T04-S003]). **Count: ≥4 (strong).**
- **争议**: heavy overlap with SPIN (it *is* SPIN's implication logic, re-marketed) — see "宗教化" note. Keenan's brash brand polarizes. (evidence: [T04-S001])
- **继承关系**: direct descendant of SPIN (Rackham) and Solution Selling (Bosworth/Eades pain chain). (evidence: [T04-S001, T04-S020])
- **可信度**: high. **Decay risk**: low.

### 挑战教学派 (teach: reframe the customer)

#### 📖 2. The Challenger Sale — Matthew Dixon & Brent Adamson (2011)

- **Authors**: Matthew Dixon & Brent Adamson (+ CEB colleagues). With Track 01 figures.
- **Year**: 2011 (Portfolio/Penguin). 1M+ copies; #1 Amazon & WSJ bestseller. (evidence: [T04-S003])
- **One-liner**: The book that dethroned pure relationship-selling — the best reps *teach* customers a new view of their problem and take control of the conversation.
- **核心论点 (3-5)**:
  1. CEB profiled reps into 5 types; the **Challenger** (teaches, tailors, takes control) is the top performer, especially in complex sales. (evidence: [T04-S003, T04-S004])
  2. **Commercial insight** — teach the customer something non-obvious about their own cost/risk — is the core skill. (evidence: [T04-S004])
  3. **Constructive tension** (not avoiding disagreement) drives change; relationship-building alone underperforms. (evidence: [T04-S004])
- **读完得到什么**: A framework for insight-led, executive-level selling + why "being liked" isn't enough.
- **难度**: 进阶. **如果只读 1 章**: the rep-profiles + commercial-insight chapters.
- **Endorsement evidence**: `[type: course_syllabus]` Challenger Inc is a full training/cert business built on it (evidence: [T04-S004]); `[type: blog_secondary]` 1M+ copies, #1 WSJ bestseller, ubiquitous in comparisons (evidence: [T04-S003]); `[type: conf_citation]` adopted by leading B2B orgs and taught alongside MEDDIC at Force Management (evidence: [T04-S023]). **Count: ≥5 (strong).**
- **争议 (keep, don't sanitize)**: The original CEB study faced a **peer-reviewed academic critique** — Rapp, Bachrach, Panagopoulos & Ogilvie (2014), *JPSSM* 34(4):245-259 (2014 Marvin Jolson Award) — questioning the "knowledge broker" model's novelty and the inference from correlation; a 2019 critique (Inks, Avila & Talbert) followed. Practitioners also note Challenger is *hard to execute*: it demands deep buyer-industry knowledge + the nerve to push back on executives, a combination most reps can't pull off authentically — so org-wide rollout often fails. (evidence: [T04-S032])
- **继承关系**: builds on insight/provocation selling; its sequel **The Challenger Customer (2015)** moves the unit of analysis from rep to **buying group** (Mobilizers/Talkers/Blockers). (evidence: [T04-S005])
- **可信度**: high (as a widely-adopted methodology). **Decay risk**: low-medium (the data is contested, the practice endures).

#### 📖 3. The JOLT Effect — Matthew Dixon & Ted McKenna (2022)

- **Authors**: Matthew Dixon & Ted McKenna. With Track 01 figures.
- **Year**: 2022 (Portfolio/Penguin). Based on a study of ~2.5M sales conversations (Tethr). (evidence: [T04-S006])
- **One-liner**: Reframes the central problem of modern selling — your enemy isn't the competitor, it's the customer's **indecision**.
- **核心论点 (3-5)**:
  1. 40-60% of forecasted deals die in "no decision" limbo; of those, ~56% *wanted to move* but couldn't commit (not status-quo preference). (evidence: [T04-S006, T04-S007])
  2. **FOMU > FOMO**: once intent exists, buyers fear *messing up* more than missing out. (evidence: [T04-S006])
  3. The **JOLT** method: **J**udge the indecision, **O**ffer a recommendation, **L**imit the exploration, **T**ake risk off the table. (evidence: [T04-S006, T04-S007])
- **读完得到什么**: A concrete playbook for the late-stage stall — the single biggest leak in enterprise pipeline.
- **难度**: 进阶. **Endorsement evidence**: `[type: figure_long]` Dixon/McKenna long-form research + JOLT site (evidence: [T04-S006]); `[type: blog_secondary]` widely reviewed/summarized as the definitive "no decision" text (evidence: [T04-S007]); `[type: figure_short]` the 40-60% no-decision stat is now industry-standard shorthand (evidence: [T04-S006]). **Count: ≥4 (strong).**
- **争议**: same lineage as Challenger (CEB/Gartner DNA), so inherits the "vendor research → book → training" pipeline skepticism; the 2.5M-conversation figure is a Tethr/proprietary dataset (not independently auditable). Numbers → caveat. (evidence: [T04-S006])
- **继承关系**: direct successor to Challenger; shifts focus from *winning vs rivals* to *defeating inaction*. (evidence: [T04-S004])
- **可信度**: high. **Decay risk**: low.

### 资格纪律派 (qualify: no bad deals)

#### 📖 4. MEDDICC — Andy Whyte (2020)  &  📖 5. The Qualified Sales Leader — John McMahon (2021)

- **Authors**: Andy Whyte (MEDDICC, 2020, foreword by MEDDIC co-creators Dick Dunkel & Jack Napoli); John McMahon (The Qualified Sales Leader, 2021). With Track 01 figures.
- **Origin of the framework**: MEDDIC was created **inside PTC in 1996** by Dick Dunkel with Jack Napoli, under SVP **John McMahon** — described as "John McMahon's sales brain codified." Later gained a 2nd **C** (Competition) and **P** (Paper Process) → MEDDPICC. (evidence: [T04-S008])
- **One-liner**: The qualification religion — a disciplined checklist to decide *which deals are real*, so reps stop wasting cycles ("no deal beats a bad deal").
- **核心论点 (3-5)**:
  1. **MEDDPICC** = Metrics, Economic buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Paper Process, Identify Pain, Champion, Competition — non-negotiable info to advance a complex deal. (evidence: [T04-S009, T04-S024])
  2. It's a **qualification framework, not a full methodology** — it tells you *what to know*, not *how to run discovery* (pairs with SPIN/Challenger/Command of the Message). (evidence: [T04-S024])
  3. McMahon's book extends it to **frontline management + forecasting**: inspect deals against MEDDPICC, coach Champions, forecast from evidence not optimism. (evidence: [T04-S008])
- **读完得到什么**: A shared deal-inspection language + the discipline to disqualify early.
- **难度**: 入门-进阶 (Whyte) / 进阶-高阶 (McMahon). **如果只读 1 章**: the Economic Buyer + Champion chapters.
- **Endorsement evidence**: `[type: figure_long]` McMahon co-hosts the Revenue Builders podcast around this worldview (evidence: [T04-S036]); `[type: course_syllabus]` taught by Force Management, MEDDIC Academy, MEDDICC.com as the enterprise cert standard (evidence: [T04-S023, T04-S037]); `[type: blog_secondary]` MEDDICC is the #1 / highest-rated MEDDIC book (4.7★, 100k+ sold) (evidence: [T04-S009, T04-S041]). **Count: ≥5 (strong).**
- **争议 (keep)**: MEDDIC's #1 failure mode is **box-checking theater** — reps fill CRM fields without truly qualifying; the framework becomes a reporting ritual, not a thinking tool. Critics also note it's a *qualification* layer wrongly sold as a complete sales method. (evidence: [T04-S024])
- **继承关系**: descends from PTC's real-world deal post-mortems; today the spine of the enterprise/SaaS "qualification-first" school. Force Management's Command of the Message is its narrative complement. (evidence: [T04-S008, T04-S023])
- **可信度**: high. **Decay risk**: low.

### SaaS 现代收入架构派 (modern GTM / revenue architecture)

#### 📖 7. Predictable Revenue — Aaron Ross & Marylou Tyler (2011)

- **Authors**: Aaron Ross & Marylou Tyler. With Track 01 figures.
- **Year**: 2011. "The Sales Bible of Silicon Valley." (evidence: [T04-S012])
- **One-liner**: The book that invented the modern SaaS sales org — split prospecting from closing and you build a predictable revenue machine.
- **核心论点 (3-5)**:
  1. **Specialize roles**: SDRs (cold outreach → qualified leads) hand off to AEs (closing) and CSMs (retention) — don't make one rep do all three. (evidence: [T04-S012])
  2. **Cold Calling 2.0**: short personalized emails asking for a referral to the right person, no cold dials. (evidence: [T04-S012])
  3. This system added ~$100M recurring revenue at Salesforce; predictability comes from process, not heroics. (evidence: [T04-S012])
- **读完得到什么**: The blueprint for the SDR→AE→CSM funnel that dominates SaaS GTM.
- **难度**: 入门. **Endorsement evidence**: `[type: figure_long]` Ross built Predictable Revenue Inc + the sequel From Impossible to Inevitable (with Jason Lemkin) (evidence: [T04-S012, T04-S013]); `[type: blog_secondary]` "sales bible of Silicon Valley," foundational reference for SaaS GTM (evidence: [T04-S012]); `[type: course_syllabus]` the SDR/AE split is now assumed in Winning by Design + Sales Development Playbook (evidence: [T04-S015, T04-S030]). **Count: ≥5 (strong).**
- **争议 (keep)**: the **SDR-army model is now itself contested** — Winning by Design, signal-based GTM (Clay), and the 2024-25 AI-SDR + email-deliverability backlash argue the assembly-line model is dying / spammy. High decay on the *tactics*, low decay on the *role-specialization principle*. (evidence: [T04-S015])
- **继承关系**: ancestor of the entire SaaS revenue-architecture school (Roberge's Sales Acceleration Formula, van der Kooij's bowtie, Bertuzzi's SDR playbook). (evidence: [T04-S014, T04-S015, T04-S030])
- **可信度**: high. **Decay risk**: medium (tactics), low (org model).

**主要分歧 (still-debated within the canon)**:
- **ask vs teach**: SPIN/Gap ("the customer has the answers, ask") ⇄ Challenger ("the customer is wrong about their problem, teach"). This is the deepest, most real schism — and the most misused (Challenger ≠ argue; SPIN ≠ interrogate). (evidence: [T04-S001, T04-S004])

### 核心概念 → 候选 playbook (single-source → playbook, not mental model)

- **Implication/Need-payoff (SPIN)** → "If discovery feels shallow, ask what the problem *costs* and what *solving it* is worth — let them quantify it." (evidence: [T04-S001])
- **JOLT** → "If a qualified deal stalls late, the issue is indecision: judge it, recommend, limit options, de-risk." (evidence: [T04-S006])
- **MEDDPICC** → "Before forecasting a deal, confirm you can name the Economic Buyer, the Champion, the Decision Process, and the Paper Process — gaps = risk." (evidence: [T04-S009, T04-S024])

### 方法论『宗教化』与重叠批判 (honest critique — DO NOT sanitize)

The single most important honest note for Phase 2.8: **SPIN / Challenger / Gap / Solution / MEDDIC overlap heavily** — all of them reduce to *discovery + pain + qualify + value + multithread* — **yet each markets itself as a unique, proprietary system, and each sells training/certs/consulting behind it** (Huthwaite, Challenger Inc, A Sales Growth Co, Sandler, MEDDIC Academy, Force Management, Winning by Design). The branded-framework economy creates an incentive to overstate novelty and package methodology as a silver bullet. Specific, evidence-backed criticisms to preserve:

- **Challenger's CEB data faced peer-reviewed replicability/causation critique** (Rapp, Bachrach, Panagopoulos & Ogilvie, *JPSSM* 2014; Inks et al. 2019) — "best reps are Challengers" is contested, not settled. (evidence: [T04-S032])
- **MEDDIC degenerates into CRM box-checking theater** — filling fields ≠ real qualification. (evidence: [T04-S024])
- **Predictable Revenue's SDR-army is now argued to be obsolete/spammy** (AI-SDR backlash, signal-based GTM). (evidence: [T04-S015])
- **Vendor research → book → cert pipeline**: JOLT/Challenger/Sales Acceleration numbers come from proprietary datasets (Tethr, CEB, HubSpot) that aren't independently auditable — treat stats as directional. (evidence: [T04-S006, T04-S004])
- **The methodologies are complementary layers, not competitors** the way their marketing implies: MEDDIC (qualify) + SPIN/Gap (discover) + Challenger (insight) + Command of the Message (articulate value) + SPICED (handoff) is how strong orgs actually blend them. The "one true methodology" framing is itself the critique. (evidence: [T04-S024])
