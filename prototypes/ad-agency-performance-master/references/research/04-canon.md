# Track 04 — Canon: 广告外包公司绩效管理 / Ad Agency Performance Management

> locale=zh-CN 受众 / **en-primary canon**. profile=practitioner (老板/COO/HR/项目总监/team lead).
> **NOT fully cold** — but the canon is *borrowed*, not native. There is **no single book titled "how to do performance
> management in an ad agency."** The working canon is assembled from four imported bodies of knowledge:
> (1) **专业服务公司(PSF)经济学** — David Maister (agency = leveraged human-capital firm; utilization/leverage/realization/margin
> are the real, measurable levers); (2) **agency 专家经营/定位定价** — David C. Baker + Blair Enns (most "performance"
> problems are actually positioning & pricing problems — fix the top line, don't just squeeze people); (3) **目标/结果管理**
> — Drucker(MBO) → Grove/Doerr(OKR) → Kaplan-Norton(BSC) → 华为(PBC), the "what gets measured gets managed" school;
> (4) **反绩效主义/内在动机** — Deming(废除考评) / Pink(Drive) / Muller(度量之暴政) / 天外伺郎(索尼), the counter-canon that
> says hard metrics on creative/knowledge work destroy the very creativity you sell.
>
> **The irreducible split** (this industry's version of the intake's 元矛盾 "创意不可量化 ⇄ 必须可考核"):
> **量化考核派 (KPI/OKR/BSC/提成/强制分布) ⇄ 反绩效主义/内在动机派 (Deming/Pink/Muller/Sony)** — arbitrated in practice by
> Maister's insight that *some* things ARE cleanly measurable (utilization, realization, project margin) even when *creative
> quality* is not, and by Baker/Enns's insight that *pricing power* (positioning) makes the whole performance math easier.
> en is the primary canon language; independent zh-CN agency-specific 一手 is thin and mostly sits on blacklisted platforms
> (知乎/公众号/HR 培训站) — a 诚实边界 input for Phase 2.8, **not** an industry-thinness signal.

## 详细 Canon 条目 — Irreducible Core（按派别）

### A. 专业服务公司经济学派 (PSF economics — "agency = leveraged human-capital firm; some things ARE measurable")

#### 📖 1. Managing the Professional Service Firm — David Maister (1993)

- **Author**: David Maister (ex-HBS professor; the foundational theorist of PSF management). With Track 01 figures.
- **Year**: 1993 (Free Press / Simon & Schuster; still in print, never superseded). (evidence: [T04-S003])
- **Type**: management monograph — the genre-defining text.
- **One-liner**: An ad agency, like a law/consulting/accounting firm, is a **leveraged human-capital business**; its performance economics are governed by a small set of measurable levers — and creative agencies borrow their appraisal logic from here whether they know it or not.
- **核心论点 (3-5)**:
  1. Profit-per-partner = **Leverage × Utilization × Billing rate × Realization × Margin** (the "LUBRM" engine). Most of the profit comes from **leverage** — hiring juniors at salary X and billing them at a multiple of X. (evidence: [T04-S001])
  2. The firm's **shape** (junior:manager:senior mix) must match its **work mix** — "Brains" (top-mind, low-leverage), "Grey-hair" (experience), "Procedure" (process-heavy, high-leverage) projects. Mismatch = broken economics. (evidence: [T04-S001])
  3. **Utilization/chargeability is a short-run lever** ("the donkey strategy — achieving more by pulling a heavier load"); the long-run levers are leverage, rate, and margin. Working people harder is the weakest move. (evidence: [T04-S001])
  4. Roles split into **Finders / Minders / Grinders** — rainmaking, project-managing, doing — each needs a different reward logic. (evidence: [T04-S001])
- **读完得到什么**: The vocabulary and the model for why an agency makes (or loses) money on people — utilization/realization/margin/leverage — the measurable half of "agency performance."
- **难度**: 进阶. **如果只读 1 章**: the leverage + the "shape of the firm" chapters.
- **Endorsement evidence**: `[type: canon_status]` universally cited as "the PSF bible" across consulting/law/accounting/agencies (evidence: [T04-S001, T04-S003]); `[type: figure_long]` Maister's full free article library at davidmaister.com keeps the ideas live (evidence: [T04-S002]); `[type: derivative]` every agency-finance vendor (Parakeeto/Productive) rebuilds utilization/leverage/margin from this base (evidence: [T04-S019, T04-S020]). **Count: ≥5 (strong).**
- **争议 (keep)**: (a) written for hourly-billing PSFs (law/consulting) — the **billable-hours/timesheet foundation is itself contested** (Ron Baker/VeraSage: "no client buys time"; value pricing is the counter) [T04-S026, T04-S027]; (b) the LUBRM formula is **descriptive, not a management dashboard** — critics ("Beware of LUBRM") warn firms game one lever at the expense of others; (c) creative-agency work is *more* project-lumpy and *less* hourly-standardized than law/consulting, so utilization targets transfer imperfectly. (evidence: [T04-S001, T04-S027])
- **继承关系**: the ancestor of essentially all agency-economics content; David C. Baker's operational work (Secret Tradecraft) is the agency-specific descendant; the Chinese 阿米巴/华为 tools independently rediscover its "responsibility profit-center" core. (evidence: [T04-S005, T04-S022])
- **可信度**: high. **Decay risk**: low (levers), medium (the hourly-billing assumption).

### B. Agency 专家经营 / 定位定价派 (positioning & pricing first — "most performance problems are pricing problems")

#### 📖 2. The Business of Expertise + 📖 3. Secret Tradecraft of Elite Advisors — David C. Baker (2017 / 2022)

- **Author**: David C. Baker — advisor to 1,000+ agencies/creative/marketing/PR/digital firms; co-host of 2Bobs; the closest thing the agency world has to an economics benchmarker (punctuation.com). With Track 01 figures.
- **Years**: *The Business of Expertise* 2017; *Secret Tradecraft of Elite Advisors* 2022. (evidence: [T04-S004, T04-S005])
- **One-liner**: An agency's performance ceiling is set upstream of any KPI — by its **positioning**. Narrow, vertical, few-substitutes positioning → pricing power → healthy margins → you can afford to pay well and appraise humanely. Weak positioning → commodity pricing → you squeeze people to survive.
- **核心论点 (3-5)**:
  1. **Isolate patterns → build insight → position narrowly (vertical > horizontal) so you have few substitutes.** Positioning is the root cause of profitability, not an afterthought. (evidence: [T04-S004])
  2. **"The more insight you publish for free, the less you need to invest in the sale; the better your positioning, the easier it is to give insight away."** Lead generation = disseminate insight + one high-impact tactic. (evidence: [T04-S005])
  3. *Secret Tradecraft* is the **operational field manual** — staffing, roles, engagement management, money — for building a "$1M+ per person" practice; it's where positioning meets day-to-day agency operations. (evidence: [T04-S005])
- **读完得到什么**: The reframe that "our people underperform" is often "our positioning is weak so our pricing is weak so our economics force overwork" — plus operational benchmarks for a healthy shop.
- **难度**: 进阶. **Endorsement evidence**: `[type: figure_long]` 2Bobs is the #1 creative-services podcast (evidence: [T04-S007]); `[type: canon_status]` Baker/Enns are the agency-management canon pair, cross-cited constantly (evidence: [T04-S007, T04-S008]); `[type: derivative]` his benchmarking (punctuation.com) is the de-facto agency-economics reference (evidence: [T04-S006]). **Count: ≥4 (strong).**
- **争议 (keep)**: Baker's advice is calibrated to **small/mid boutique expert firms in mature markets** (US/EU); the "verticalize, raise prices, fire bad-fit clients" playbook is harder in China's low-price 内卷 red ocean where 甲方 holds power and 比稿 white-labor is normalized. Transfer with caveat. (evidence: [T04-S004, T04-S005])
- **继承关系**: operationalizes Maister's PSF economics for the modern independent agency; pairs with Enns (positioning → the pricing that positioning enables). (evidence: [T04-S001, T04-S009])
- **可信度**: high (for the boutique-agency world). **Decay risk**: low.

#### 📖 4. The Win Without Pitching Manifesto + 📖 5. Pricing Creativity — Blair Enns (2010 / 2018)

- **Author**: Blair Enns — ex-adman, founder of Win Without Pitching (2002), co-host of 2Bobs; the authority on selling/pricing for creative firms. With Track 01 figures.
- **Years**: *Manifesto* 2010 (100k+ sold); *Pricing Creativity: A Guide to Profit Beyond the Billable Hour* 2018; *The Four Conversations* 2022. (evidence: [T04-S008, T04-S009])
- **One-liner**: The **top-line discipline** that makes agency performance math work — stop giving creativity away in free pitches, and price the **value to the client, not your hours**.
- **核心论点 (3-5)**:
  1. **Refuse the free pitch / spec work.** Pitching commoditizes the agency and destroys margin; sell from a position of expertise/authority instead. (evidence: [T04-S008])
  2. **Value-based pricing over hourly**: "charge based on the value to the client, not the inputs of time and material." The billable hour caps your upside and rewards slowness. (evidence: [T04-S009]; converges with Ron Baker [T04-S026])
  3. **Present three options, lead with the highest ("what would we do if money were no object?")** — anchoring raises the average settled price. (evidence: [T04-S009])
- **读完得到什么**: The pricing/pitching operating system that determines whether there's enough margin to reward creative people well — the lever most agencies neglect while obsessing over internal KPIs.
- **难度**: 入门-进阶. **Endorsement evidence**: `[type: canon_status]` Manifesto is foundational reading for creative firms, 100k+ sold (evidence: [T04-S008, T04-S009]); `[type: figure_long]` 2Bobs + Win Without Pitching program (evidence: [T04-S007]); `[type: derivative]` value-pricing lineage shared with Ron Baker/VeraSage (evidence: [T04-S026]). **Count: ≥5 (strong).**
- **争议 (keep)**: value-pricing/no-pitch is **hardest exactly where agencies are weakest** — in commoditized markets with procurement-driven, price-shopping 甲方 who *require* pitches. The "just say no to pitching" advice assumes negotiating leverage many乙方 don't have (a core zh-CN transfer caveat). (evidence: [T04-S008])
- **继承关系**: the pricing complement to Baker's positioning; both descend from the PSF "sell expertise, not hours" worldview and feed the anti-timesheet camp (Ron Baker). (evidence: [T04-S004, T04-S026])
- **可信度**: high. **Decay risk**: low.

### C. 目标 / 结果管理派 (goal & results management — "what gets measured gets managed")

#### 📖 6. Measure What Matters — John Doerr (2018) [+ MBO/Drucker ancestor + BSC/Kaplan-Norton sibling + 华为 PBC cousin]

- **Author**: John Doerr (Kleiner Perkins; learned OKRs from Andy Grove at Intel, brought them to Google in 1999). With Track 01 figures.
- **Year**: 2018 (Portfolio/Penguin). (evidence: [T04-S010])
- **One-liner**: The modern, tech-borrowed goal system agencies reach for when KPIs feel too rigid — **Objectives (qualitative "what") + Key Results (measurable "how we'll know")**, ideally *decoupled from compensation*.
- **核心论点 (3-5)**:
  1. Formula: **"I will (Objective) as measured by (Key Results)"** — 3-5 KRs per Objective; ~70% attainment = healthily ambitious ("stretch"). (evidence: [T04-S011])
  2. **Four superpowers**: Focus (commit to what matters), Align (transparent, cross-org), Track (adapt), Stretch (aim high). (evidence: [T04-S010])
  3. **CFRs (Conversations, Feedback, Recognition)** are the human layer that makes OKRs work — "continuous performance management" that Doerr contrasts with the dreaded annual review. (evidence: [T04-S010])
  4. OKRs are best when **separated from pay** — mixing stretch goals with bonus math makes people sandbag. (evidence: [T04-S010])
- **读完得到什么**: The process/growth pole of the tension — align creative teams on qualitative objectives without pretending creativity is a number.
- **难度**: 入门. **Endorsement evidence**: `[type: canon_status]` the defining OKR text, Google/Gates/Bono case studies (evidence: [T04-S010]); `[type: course_syllabus]` whatmatters.com runs a free OKR course + examples (evidence: [T04-S011]); `[type: local_adoption]` 字节/飞书 popularized OKR in China 2019-2023 (see decay note). **Count: ≥5 (strong).**
- **争议 (keep)**: (a) **OKR in China peaked ~2019-2023 then cooled** — many firms bolted OKR onto a KPI/绩效奖金 culture, breaking the "decouple from pay" rule and getting KPI-in-disguise (时效 decay, medium); (b) "向甲方/大厂看齐做 OKR" often **水土不服** in small agencies; (c) qualitative Objectives can become vague theater. (evidence: [T04-S010])
- **继承关系**: **Drucker's MBO (1954)** is the grandparent (Objectives + self-control; commitment > compliance); **Grove/Intel** the parent; **Kaplan-Norton's Balanced Scorecard (1992)** the balanced-metrics sibling (4 perspectives so you don't over-optimize financials); **华为 PBC** the Chinese enterprise cousin (import from IBM). (evidence: [T04-S011, T04-S012, T04-S024])
- **可信度**: high. **Decay risk**: medium (the China OKR hype cycle).

#### 📖 6b. The Balanced Scorecard — Robert Kaplan & David Norton (1992) & MBO — Peter Drucker (1954) [foundational siblings]

- **BSC one-liner**: Don't manage on financials alone — balance **Financial / Customer / Internal Process / Learning & Growth**, linked by a **strategy map** of cause-and-effect. For agencies: 回款/利润 (financial) + 客户满意/续约 (customer) + 交付/返稿/利用率 (process) + 人才培养/流失 (learning). (evidence: [T04-S012])
- **MBO one-liner**: Drucker's *Management by Objectives **and self-control*** (1954) — the ancestor of KPI/OKR/BSC. Its **most-forgotten half is "self-control"**: participation produces commitment that compliance cannot. Every modern goal tool that becomes top-down box-checking has lost Drucker's point. (evidence: [T04-S011] lineage)
- **争议 (keep)**: BSC can bloat into a 20-metric dashboard nobody acts on; MBO "fails (and so may OKR)" when objectives are imposed, not owned. Both are only as good as the honesty of the conversation behind them. (evidence: [T04-S012])
- **可信度**: high (foundational). **Decay risk**: low.

### D. 反绩效主义 / 内在动机派 (the counter-canon — "hard metrics on creative work destroy what you're selling")

> This is the pole the intake calls out as most-often over-simplified. Keep it sharp — it is not "creativity is sacred,"
> it is a **specific, evidence-backed argument** that measurement + high stakes on creative/knowledge work is
> self-defeating. It is the necessary counterweight to the whole 量化考核 machine.

#### 📖 7. Drive — Daniel Pink (2009)

- **One-liner**: For creative/cognitive work, **carrots-and-sticks backfire** — motivation comes from **Autonomy, Mastery, Purpose**, and extrinsic rewards crowd out intrinsic drive. (evidence: [T04-S015])
- **核心论点**: (1) "If the task involved cognitive skills, decision-making, creativity, or higher-order thinking, **higher pay resulted in lower performance**." (2) Autonomy (control over what/when/how/who) + Mastery (getting better at what matters) + Purpose (contribution) are the real fuel. (3) Extrinsic "if-then" rewards suit routine tasks, harm creative ones. (evidence: [T04-S015])
- **争议/adjacency**: builds on **Deci/Ryan Self-Determination Theory** and **Herzberg's two-factor** (adjacent foundational — money is a "hygiene" factor, not a satisfier); critics say Pink over-flattens the research, but the creative-task finding is robust. **Direct hit on 提成制**: pure commission on creative roles can *reduce* creative quality. (evidence: [T04-S015])
- **可信度**: high (for the creative-task claim). **Decay risk**: low.

#### 📖 8. Deming on Performance Appraisal (Out of the Crisis, 1982) & 📖 The Tyranny of Metrics — Jerry Muller (2018)

- **Deming one-liner**: **Abolish annual appraisal and merit rating** — it is one of the "seven deadly diseases." "The system that people work in… may account for **90-95%** of performance"; appraisal "necessarily and inappropriately attributes variation to individuals rather than the system managers built." It "nourishes short-term performance, annihilates long-term planning, builds fear, demolishes teamwork, nourishes rivalry." (evidence: [T04-S014])
- **Muller one-liner**: **Goodhart's Law** — "**when a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure**." Once high stakes attach to a metric, people game it, optimizing the number at the expense of the unmeasured (and often more important) goal. "Metric fixation" = measuring/publicizing/rewarding the wrong things because they're easy to count. (evidence: [T04-S016])
- **Why they matter for agencies**: together with Pink they form the case that **individual KPIs + forced ranking + commission on creative work** will (a) misattribute team/system outcomes to individuals, (b) get gamed (chase billable hours, sandbag stretch, cherry-pick easy clients), and (c) kill the collaboration a creative product requires. This is the **first-principles objection** to naive quantification. (evidence: [T04-S014, T04-S016])
- **争议 (keep — do NOT sanitize)**: the counter-view (Maister/agency-KPI camp) is that **without any measurement you get 养懒人 and owners who can't see the P&L** — utilization, realization, and project margin *are* legitimately measurable even if creative quality isn't. The honest resolution is the **三层拆分** (quality/efficiency/results), not "abolish all metrics." (evidence: [T04-S001, T04-S021])
- **可信度**: high. **Decay risk**: low.

#### ⚠️ 案例经典 — 《绩效主义毁了索尼》天外伺郎 (2007) [the cautionary tale, contradiction preserved]

- **The thesis**: former Sony director 天外伺郎, in 文艺春秋 (Jan 2007), argued 绩效主义 (tying results directly to money) killed Sony's "**激情集团**" (passion groups), its challenge spirit, and its teamwork — blaming it for ~4 years of losses (framed as ~$6.3B). "外在动机增强，自发动机就受抑制。" (evidence: [T04-S018])
- **The counter-critique (keep both sides)**: credible re-examinations (界面新闻) argue this is **partly myth** — what Sony ran was arguably **考核主义 (rating-for-pay), not true 绩效主义**; and Sony's decline had many causes (missed digital/mobile transitions, silos, yen, competition), not just appraisal. The article became a rhetorical weapon in China's anti-KPI camp. (evidence: [T04-S018])
- **Why it's canon here**: it is the single most-cited *story* in the Chinese "KPI 杀创意" debate — **you must know it and its debunking**. Present as: *directionally illustrative of the intrinsic-motivation risk, factually contested as a monocausal explanation.* (evidence: [T04-S018])

### E. 教练 / 发展派 (develop, don't rank)

#### 📖 9. Coaching for Performance (GROW) — John Whitmore (1992)

- **One-liner**: Performance comes from **coaching conversations**, not ratings — the **GROW** model (Goal / Reality / Options / Will) structures the 绩效面谈 as development, not judgment. (evidence: [T04-S013])
- **核心论点**: (1) awareness + responsibility (from Gallwey's *Inner Game*) drive performance; (2) GROW = ask, don't tell — surface the coachee's own Goal, Reality, Options, and Will; (3) the manager-as-coach beats the manager-as-judge. (evidence: [T04-S013])
- **Endorsement**: 1M+ copies, 20+ languages, 6th ed. 2024; founded the modern (billion-dollar) performance-coaching industry. (evidence: [T04-S013])
- **Why it matters for agencies**: bridges the two poles — a *humane* way to run the 绩效面谈 / PIP / 1:1 that develops creative talent instead of ranking it; the operational answer to "if not forced ranking, then what?" (evidence: [T04-S013])
- **可信度**: high. **Decay risk**: low.

World-view: *make every small unit/person a profit-accountable, profit-sharing actor (多劳多得/利益共享).* Caveat: heavy training-industry packaging; take mechanism, not the "one system cures all" pitch. (evidence: [T04-S022, T04-S025, T04-S023, T04-S024])
- **反绩效主义/内在动机派 (anti-metric / intrinsic motivation)** — **Deming (abolish appraisal, 1982)**, **Pink (Drive, 2009)**, **Muller (Tyranny of Metrics, 2018)**, **天外伺郎 (Sony, 2007)**; 血缘: Herzberg/SDT (adjacent). World-view: *hard metrics + high stakes on creative/knowledge work destroy motivation, teamwork, and the creativity you sell.* (evidence: [T04-S014, T04-S015, T04-S016, T04-S018])
- **教练/发展派 (coach, don't rank)** — **Whitmore (Coaching for Performance / GROW, 1992)**; 当前: Doerr's CFRs. World-view: *performance is grown through conversation, not manufactured through rating.* (evidence: [T04-S013, T04-S010])

**主要分歧 (still-debated within the canon — keep unsoftened)**:
- **量化 vs 反量化**: KPI/OKR/BSC/提成/强制分布 ("不考核养懒人、老板算不清账") ⇄ Deming/Pink/Muller/Sony ("硬考核杀创意、毁协作、被博弈"). The industry's deepest schism; resolved *only* by the quality/efficiency/results 三层拆分, never by picking a side. (evidence: [T04-S001, T04-S014, T04-S021])
- **结果 vs 过程**: KPI/业绩/回款 (results) ⇄ OKR/能力/成长/GROW (process). (evidence: [T04-S011, T04-S013])
- **个人 vs 团队**: 提成制/forced-ranking (individual, "养蛊") ⇄ Deming/阿米巴/利益共享 (team/unit, "大锅饭" risk). (evidence: [T04-S014, T04-S022])
- **强激励 vs 稳定**: 提成制 (multi-pay, but poaching/short-termism/Pink says it *lowers* creative output) ⇄ 固定+奖金 (fair but 大锅饭). (evidence: [T04-S015, T04-S028])
- **工时制 vs 价值定价**: Maister's billable-hours PSF logic ⇄ Enns/Ron Baker "no client buys time." (evidence: [T04-S001, T04-S026])

### 核心概念 → 候选 playbook (single-source → playbook, not mental model)

- **LUBRM (Maister)** → "算不清 agency 为什么不赚钱时，逐一看 leverage(配比)/utilization(利用率)/rate(报价)/realization(回款折损)/margin——先提 rate，最后才逼 utilization。" (evidence: [T04-S001])
- **3 选项高锚定价 (Enns)** → "报价永远给三档、领读最贵那档('钱不是问题会怎么做')——平均成交价会上移。" (evidence: [T04-S009])
- **OKR 与薪酬脱钩 (Doerr)** → "把有挑战的 Objective 和奖金分开，否则员工会把目标定低(sandbag)。" (evidence: [T04-S010])
- **单位时间核算 (阿米巴)** → "把团队变小核算单元，盯'(收入−费用)÷总工时'的单位时间附加值，让每个组像小老板。" (evidence: [T04-S022])
- **GROW 绩效面谈 (Whitmore)** → "面谈按 Goal→Reality→Options→Will 提问，别给判决——让创意人自己说出差距和下一步。" (evidence: [T04-S013])
- **Goodhart 反身检查 (Muller)** → "上任何硬 KPI 前问：这个数被挂高利害后会怎样被钻空子？(改稿刷工时、抢易做的客户、注水续约)" (evidence: [T04-S016])

### 方法论『宗教化』与重叠批判 (honest critique — DO NOT sanitize)

- **中式工具的"万能药"营销**: KSF/积分制/阿米巴/华为PBC 在中文培训圈被包装成"一套体系包治百病"，且大量二手洗稿(全在黑名单平台)。它们的**真实机理其实与 MBO/KPI/利润中心/利益共享 高度重叠**——取机理、标 surrogate/reference、不背书培训机构。KSF "源自哈佛"的 pedigree 尤其存疑。(evidence: [T04-S025, T04-S023, T04-S022])
- **量化派的商业动机**: agency-finance vendors (Parakeeto/Productive/北森/Moka/飞书) 卖工具，天然放大"一切皆可量化/可仪表盘化"；数字类 benchmark 是厂商口径，须 caveat。(evidence: [T04-S019, T04-S020])
- **反量化派的过度简化**: "创意神圣不可考核"会滑向养懒人；Deming/Pink/Muller 的严谨论点常被断章取义成"别管数字"。真正的分歧是**哪些层可量化**，不是"量不量化"。(evidence: [T04-S014, T04-S001])
- **Sony 神话**: 《绩效主义毁了索尼》是修辞武器，单因论已被证伪——引用须同时给出反驳。(evidence: [T04-S018])
- **乙方绩效的本质(最重要的一条)**: 甲乙方权力不对等(压价/无限改稿/比稿白嫖/账期垫款)会**系统性侵蚀** agency 利润与员工绩效——所以 agency 绩效很大程度是"**用内部考核对冲甲方不确定性的转嫁**"。Baker/Enns 的定位定价正是想夺回这份 leverage；这层不能写成脱离甲乙方关系的纯 HR 绩效。(evidence: [T04-S004, T04-S008])
